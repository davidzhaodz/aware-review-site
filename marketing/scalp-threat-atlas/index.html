What This Means for Your Hair

Four environmental factors work together to create scalp stress. Understanding your local threats is the first step toward protecting your hair.

UV Exposure

UV radiation penetrates the hair cuticle, breaking down keratin proteins and melanin. This creates free radicals that continue damaging hair long after sun exposure ends, leading to dryness, brittleness, and color fading.

Water Hardness

Calcium and magnesium ions in hard water bond to the hair shaft, creating a rough, scaly coating that blocks moisture and products. With UV exposure, these minerals oxidize and can amplify damage.

Air Quality

Particulate matter from pollution settles onto the scalp and hair, creating a layer that blocks follicles, triggers inflammation, and attracts more dirt and oil. This leads to excess oil production and scalp irritation.

Humidity

High humidity causes hair to absorb excess moisture, breaking hydrogen bonds and creating frizz, while also promoting fungal growth. Low humidity strips moisture, making hair brittle and prone to static and breakage.

Methodology

Scores reflect composite environmental stress from five factors, weighted by their impact on scalp health.

Scoring Formula

Overall Percentile = UV (35%) + Water Hardness (30%) + Air Quality (20%) + Humidity (15%). Environmental Stress Days (ESD) are calculated as the sum of all intensity factors multiplied by 125, capped at 350.

Threat Level Classification

  • Severe: 70th percentile or above
  • High: 50th to 69th percentile
  • Moderate: 30th to 49th percentile
  • Fair: 15th to 29th percentile
  • Good: Below 15th percentile

Data Sources

  • UV Index: NOAA Solar Radiation Database, annual averages
  • Water Hardness: USGS National Water Quality Assessment, state-level data (PPM)
  • Air Quality: EPA Air Quality System, annual AQI summaries
  • Humidity: NOAA National Climatic Data Center, annual averages
  • 20 metros use comprehensive annual climate data. 30 metros use climate-estimated annual data from known regional patterns.

Limitations

  • Water hardness varies within metro areas depending on local water source
  • AQI can fluctuate significantly due to wildfire seasons
  • Individual exposure depends on indoor/outdoor time and personal routine
  • This atlas provides general environmental context, not personal health advice

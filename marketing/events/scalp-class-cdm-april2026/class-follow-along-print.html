You See More Scalps Than Dermatologists

Think of the scalp like a garden. The hair is what grows above the surface, but everything depends on the soil underneath. Compared to the face, the scalp has more follicles, more sebaceous (oil) glands, more friction from brushing, styling, and tension, and it is a covered environment, warm and humid under the hair, so the microbiome shifts more easily.

Your clients sit in your chair every 4 to 6 weeks. You part their hair section by section, you are at the bowl with your hands on their scalp. You are the first to see change. That is your opportunity to catch it early and build a service around it.

2

The 4 Pillars of Scalp Health

◎

Barrier

The Scalp's "Roof"

Cells plus lipids that keep the good in and bad out. When compromised, the scalp becomes porous and reactive.

At the chair: Tightness after shampoo, stinging with color, thin red patches at the part.

◈

Microbiome

The Ecosystem

Bacteria and yeast that live on the scalp naturally. Not sterile, balanced. Problems come from shifts, not dirt.

At the chair: Recurrent flakes days after washing, waxy scaling, odor at the roots.

⚠

Inflammation

The Alarm System

An immune response. The scalp saying something is stressing it. The bridge between discomfort and hair issues.

At the chair: Tender crown, warmth, itch they can't ignore, redness along the hairline.

↻

Circulation

Traffic + Tension

Follicles need blood flow for nutrients and oxygen. When flow is restricted, follicles shrink and growth slows.

At the chair: Tight scalp, jaw tension, thinning at the crown where the galea is thickest.

What Your Clients Are Telling You

Symptom What they say

"My hair feels thinner"
"I have dandruff / flakes"
"My scalp itches"
"I have a lot of build-up"
"It burns after color"

Ask Before recommending

Any new stress or life change?
New medication or product?
Diet shifts, protein, iron?
How often do you wash?
What are you using at home?

See What you observe

Widened part or sparse crown
Dry powdery vs waxy yellow flakes
Redness or reactive patches
Build-up at nape or hairline
Up to 150 hairs / day of shedding is normal
◆

Know your scope: You are not diagnosing. You are observing, asking, and caring for the scalp environment. Refer to a dermatologist for sudden or patchy loss, large yellow flakes that stick to the scalp, visible lesions, persistent pain, or anything that looks off to you.

Scalp Check The 4 Zones to Analyze

01

Crown

Overall balance. Oil, dryness, density.

02

Hairline + Temples

Recession, breakage, tension from ponytails.

03

Sides

Is it isolated or widespread? Barrier check.

04

Nape

Build-up, congestion, tenderness.

At each zone, mentally run the 4 pillars: barrier, microbiome, inflammation, circulation. Look for patterns, not diagnoses. Does what you see match what the client is telling you?

The 4R Framework

R1

Reset

9·1 Shampoo

Clear the follicular opening. Double cleanse: first wash lifts, second treats the scalp.

Repair

9·1 Conditioner

Restore the barrier and support microbiome balance. Doubles as a scalp mask.

R3

Reinforce

9·1 Leave-In

Shield against UV, pollution, and heat. Protect the part line where damage concentrates.

R4

Reactivate

Scalp Gua Sha

Release fascia, stimulate circulation, support a healthy follicle environment.

At the Bowl Gua Sha: The 4 Steps

01

Hairline

Front to back, 8-10 strokes

02

Crown (Baihui)

Circular / radial, 8-10 strokes

03

Temples

Upward + small circles, 8-10 each

04

Nape

Upward to crown, 8-10 strokes

Credibility tip: The crown point is called Baihui in traditional Chinese medicine, "hundred meetings." Say to the client: "I'm going to work the Baihui point at your crown." Offer a 2-minute version during conditioning as a complimentary touch, or build the full sequence into a paid tier.

Key Principle: Move the scalp tissue, not just the hair. Light to medium pressure, flat surface in full contact. If it feels like scraping, ease up.
Do not gua sha when: Open sores, active infection, severe flare-ups, or immediately after a chemical service. If it causes pain, stop.

Scalp Conditions by Season

Your scalp barrier reacts to every environmental shift. You do not need four different routines, just one solid routine with seasonal adjustments.

SpringMar - May SummerJun - Aug FallSep - Nov WinterDec - Feb
Key stressor Pollen, rising humidity, allergens UV radiation, excess sweat and oil, chlorine/saltwater Dropping humidity, natural shedding cycle peaks Dry air, indoor heating (humidity can drop below 20%), static
Scalp response Increased sensitivity and reactivity, possible inflammation from pollen contact Barrier breakdown from UV oxidative stress, overactive oil production, pH shifts from sweat Telogen shedding becomes visible (Aug-Sep peak), barrier begins losing moisture Accelerated moisture loss, lipid layer gaps, compensatory oil production that can feed yeast overgrowth
Symptoms Itchiness, redness, scalp feels reactive, possible follicle congestion from heavy winter products Oily scalp, clogged follicles, sunburn along part line, dry and brittle hair from pool/ocean Increased hair in brush (normal cycle), early dryness, rougher hair texture Tightness after washing, white flaking, static-prone hair, persistent dryness
Routine adjustment Lighten leave-in products, add evening rinse on high-pollen days, transition gradually from winter routine UV protection (hats + antioxidant leave-in), water rinses between washes, pre-wet hair before swimming Reintroduce richer leave-in, support new growth with anti-inflammatory ingredients, begin moisture-sealing steps Use humidifier (40-60%), switch to richer hydrating products, reduce wash frequency, avoid hot water
The transition principle: Make one product change at a time and give it 2 to 3 weeks before adjusting further. Abrupt product changes can confuse your scalp. Start adjustments before symptoms appear, not after.

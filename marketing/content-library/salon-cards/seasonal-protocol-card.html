Seasonal Scalp Care Protocol

How to adjust recommendations throughout the year

Spring

Stressor
Rising humidity, pollen, UV increase
Scalp Response
Sebum overproduction, sensitivity, seasonal shedding (telogen release)
Adjustment
Lighter application, slightly more frequent cleansing, pollen-aware care
Focus Product
9-1 Daily Scalp Cleanser (gentle oil management)

Summer

Stressor
Peak UV, chlorine, salt water, sweat
Scalp Response
Barrier stress, oxidative damage, dehydration under heat
Adjustment
UV-protective leave-in daily, post-swim rinse protocol, hydrating mask weekly
Focus Product
9-1 Professional Leave-In (UV defense)

Fall

Stressor
Dropping humidity, temperature shift, seasonal shedding peak
Scalp Response
Increased shedding (normal fall cycle), dryness beginning, barrier transition
Adjustment
Chelating wash to clear summer buildup, increase hydration, reduce wash frequency
Focus Product
9-1 Hydrating Scalp Mask (barrier repair)

Winter

Stressor
Dry air (indoor heating), cold, static, low humidity
Scalp Response
Barrier compromise, dryness, flaking, tightness, increased sensitivity
Adjustment
Wash every 3-4 days max, mask 2x/week, avoid hot water, leave-in for moisture seal
Focus Product
Full ritual (Cleanser + Mask + Leave-In)
Transition Periods (2-4 weeks between seasons) These are the windows when clients most commonly experience sensitivity, shedding, or oiliness changes. Proactively addressing transitions during appointments builds trust and prevents reactive concerns.
"Why is my hair shedding more in fall?"
Seasonal shedding is normal. Many people lose more hair in fall as the growth cycle shifts. It typically resolves within 4-6 weeks. If it continues longer, further assessment may be warranted.

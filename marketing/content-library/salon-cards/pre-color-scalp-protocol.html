Pre-Color Scalp Protocol

4 steps to improve color consistency, longevity, and client satisfaction

The Protocol

1
Assess (2 min at consultation) - Visual inspection of 4-5 scalp zones for redness, flaking, oil patterns. Ask about water quality, product routine, medication changes. Check for contraindications (open sores, active psoriasis).
2
Prepare (10-15 min add-on) - Chelation for hard water clients. pH-balanced cleanse to normalize scalp environment. Double-cleanse technique: first wash lifts surface debris, second normalizes pH.
3
Protect - Barrier cream at hairline, ears, nape. Scalp protectant for sensitive clients. Monitor processing with scalp awareness, not just strand checks.
4
Recover - Acidic rinse to seal cuticle and restore pH. Scalp-specific conditioning (not just hair-focused). Leave-in with UV defense. The chair product IS the take-home recommendation.

Client Questions

"Why do I need a scalp treatment before color?"
Mineral buildup and pH imbalances affect how evenly color deposits. Preparing the scalp creates a consistent canvas for more predictable, longer-lasting results.
"Will this add a lot of time to my appointment?"
About 10-15 minutes. The color application and processing times stay the same. We are just optimizing the starting conditions.
Recommend: 9-1 Daily Scalp Cleanser (pre-color prep) + 9-1 Professional Leave-In (post-color recovery)
When: Client has hard water, uneven color history, scalp sensitivity, or visible mineral buildup

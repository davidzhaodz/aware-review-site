The 4R Framework for Hair Loss

A trichologist's approach to supporting clients experiencing shedding

The Framework

1
Recognize
Identify the type of shedding and potential triggers. Is it diffuse or patchy? Sudden or gradual? Ask about stress, nutrition, medication changes, hormonal shifts. Normal shedding: 50-100 hairs/day.
2
Reset
Remove environmental stressors from the scalp. Address mineral buildup, product residue, and pH disruption. Chelating cleanse + barrier assessment. This is the foundation before any restorative work.
3
Replenish
Restore the scalp barrier with targeted hydration and microbiome support. Dual-weight hyaluronic acid for deep + surface moisture. Prebiotic compounds to support healthy flora.
4
Reinforce
Protect the environment that supports healthy follicle cycling. UV defense, environmental shielding, and daily scalp barrier maintenance. This is the ongoing, long-term step.
When to Refer to a Dermatologist Patchy (not diffuse) hair loss, visible scalp through hair in previously full areas, shedding continuing beyond 6 months, shedding that started before any known trigger, or signs of scarring. These are outside scope of practice for stylists.

Client Questions

"Am I losing too much hair?"
Most people shed 50-100 hairs daily. If you are consistently seeing more, or noticing thinning at the part or temples, it is worth investigating. Many causes of increased shedding are temporary and reversible.
"Can a shampoo fix hair loss?"
No single product reverses hair loss. But supporting the scalp environment, removing buildup, restoring pH, and protecting the barrier, creates the conditions where healthy follicle cycling can resume.
Recommend: Full 9-1 ritual (Cleanser + Mask + Leave-In) + Sandalwood Gua Sha Comb for circulation
When: Client mentions increased shedding, thinning, or recent stress/medication changes

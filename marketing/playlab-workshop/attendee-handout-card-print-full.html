Your 5-Minute Scalp Gua Sha Ritual

Gua sha along your scalp's key meridian points, 3-5 minutes, 3-5x per week

◆

01

Hairline

Shenting GV24

Glide from hairline, arcing back toward the crown with gentle pressure.

Tip: Hold the flat edge almost flat against your scalp. Repeat 5-8 strokes per pass.

02

Crown

Baihui GV20

Pause at the crown. Apply light circular pressure for 10-15 seconds.

Tip: Use the rounded edge. Small circles, firm but never painful.

03

Temples

Shuaigu GB8

Sweep backward along both temples with the curved edge, following the GB meridian.

Tip: Match the curved edge to the contour of your skull. Light, even strokes.

04

Nape

Fengchi GB20

Press and hold at the base of the skull for 10-15 seconds to release tension.

Tip: Find the two hollows at the skull base. Apply steady, deep pressure.

Why Meridian Points Matter

Traditional Chinese Medicine meets modern scalp science

These four points sit along the Governing Vessel and Gallbladder meridians, two of the body's primary energy channels. Stimulating them with gua sha promotes blood flow to hair follicles, clears tension, and supports your scalp's natural renewal cycle.
GV24 - Shenting

"Spirit Courtyard"

Where the Governing Vessel crosses the hairline. Calms the mind and opens circulation along the scalp's central channel.

GV20 - Baihui

"Hundred Convergences"

The crown vertex where six meridians meet. The most important point in TCM for nourishing hair - increases local blood flow by up to 45%.

GB8 - Shuaigu

"Leading Valley"

On the Gallbladder meridian above the ear. Relieves temporal tension and promotes lateral scalp circulation.

GB20 - Fengchi

"Wind Pool"

At the base of the skull between the neck muscles. Releases stored tension and improves blood return from the scalp.

Tip: Always stroke in the direction of meridian flow - from hairline backward over the crown, then down along the sides and nape.

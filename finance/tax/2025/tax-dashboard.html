Due: Apr 15, 2026 (no extension)
  • Pay CA $800 franchise tax (TY2026) via FTB Web Pay

    • Pay CA Franchise Tax

    Every CA LLC owes a minimum $800 franchise tax each year. This is for TY2026 (not 2025), due April 15.

    1 Go to ftb.ca.gov/pay
    2 Click "Web Pay" for businesses
    3 Enter: Entity type = LLC, tax year = 2026
    4 Payment type: Annual Tax (franchise tax)
    5 Amount: $800
    6 Enter your bank account info for ACH payment
    7 Submit and save confirmation number
    The $800 franchise tax has NO extension. It's due April 15 every year regardless of any filing extensions. Late payment incurs 7% annual interest + 5% penalty.
  • Calculate Q1 2026 estimated tax per member

    • Estimated Tax Calculation

    Members may need to make estimated tax payments based on their expected 2026 income share.

    1 Estimate 2026 partnership income (use 2025 as baseline)
    2 Multiply by each member's ownership percentage
    3 Apply federal + state tax rates to each member's share
    4 Federal: Divide annual estimated tax by 4 (25/25/25/25) for quarterly payments
    5 California: Use the 30/40/0/30 schedule - Q1: 30% (Apr 15), Q2: 40% (Jun 15), Q3: $0 (Sep 15), Q4: 30% (Jan 15)
    Tip: This is each member's personal responsibility, not the LLC's. But providing the income estimates helps members calculate their payments. Safe harbor: Pay at least 100% of prior year's tax liability to avoid underpayment penalties (110% if AGI > $150K).
    CA estimated tax schedule is NOT 25/25/25/25 like federal. It's 30/40/0/30. Missing Q2's 40% payment is the most common error.
  • Notify members of estimated payment amounts

    • Member Notification

    1 Send each member their estimated 2026 income allocation
    2 Remind them of Q1 estimated payment deadline: April 15, 2026
    3 Remind them that partnership income is self-employment income (subject to SE tax)
    4 Members pay via IRS Direct Pay or EFTPS (federal) and FTB Web Pay (CA)
    5 Remind members: CA estimated tax uses 30/40/0/30 schedule (NOT 25/25/25/25 like federal)
  • Members file Form 4868 (personal extension)

    • Personal Extension (Member Responsibility)

    Since K-1s won't be ready until September, each member should file a personal extension.

    1 Each member files Form 4868 at IRS Free File
    2 This extends their personal filing deadline to October 15, 2026
    3 Estimated tax payments are still due April 15 (extension to file, not pay)
    Tip: This is informational - each member handles their own personal extension. Your job is to make sure they know they need to do it and have income estimates.

Schedule L Exemption: Schedule L (balance sheet) may NOT be required if ALL of these apply: (1) total receipts under $250,000, (2) total assets under $1,000,000, (3) K-1s filed timely, and (4) not filing Schedule M-3. CA Estimated Tax Schedule: California uses a 30/40/0/30 installment schedule for estimated taxes, NOT the federal 25/25/25/25. This means: Q1 = 30% (Apr 15), Q2 = 40% (Jun 15), Q3 = $0 (Sep 15), Q4 = 30% (Jan 15). Each member should use this schedule for their CA payments.

Nonresident Withholding: If any LLC members are NOT California residents, California requires 7% withholding on their CA-source income over $1,500/year. The LLC is responsible for withholding and remitting via Form 592 (or 592-PTE for pass-through entities). [Verify member residencies - if all CA residents, this does not apply]

CA $1M AGI Safe Harbor Rule: Members with CA AGI over $1,000,000 ($500K if married filing separately) CANNOT use the prior-year safe harbor for CA estimated taxes. They must pay 90% of current year tax. This is stricter than the federal rule. [Applies to individual members, not the LLC]

CA Statement of Information: The biennial filing with CA Secretary of State uses Form LLC-12 (not SI-100, which is for corporations). $20 online fee at bizfileonline.sos.ca.gov. Check when AWARE's next filing is due.

CA Late Payment Penalty Detail: The CA late payment penalty structure is 5% one-time initial penalty, plus 0.5% per additional month, capped at 25% total. This is NOT 5% per month as sometimes stated.

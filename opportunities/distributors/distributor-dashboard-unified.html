Objection Handling

From the perspective of a skeptical distributor brand committee evaluating AWARE.

1. "$55-75 is a tough sell for an unknown brand."

Most professional products sit $28-42 retail. AWARE is 30-60% above that without name recognition.
Counter: 96% repurchase proves salons reorder after first buy. 50% salon margin at $27.50 wholesale is same or better than most lines. Price works because clients are already seeking scalp care (18M+ organic views = inbound demand).

2. "50+ salons isn't enough to prove anything."

50+ salons in one metro doesn't prove regional or national sell-through.
Counter: Acknowledge directly: "We're early, that's why we want a distribution partner." 96% repurchase over 50+ salons is a stronger signal than 200 salons at 60% repurchase. Frame as product-market fit proof, not scale proof.

3. "We just onboarded new brands. Reps are maxed."

Onboarding costs 3-6 months of rep education time. New brand fatigue is real.
Counter: Only 3-5 SKUs, not a 20-SKU line. It's a "category add" not a "brand replacement." Scalp care doesn't compete with color, styling, or treatment lines - it's additive revenue.

4. "Scalp care is niche. Is there enough demand?"

Scalp care is still emerging in professional channel. Most distributors haven't seen sustained demand.
Counter: 18M+ organic social views proves consumer demand. Head spa trend going mainstream. Key insight: salons offering scalp services see higher spend on color and styling too - lifts the whole ticket.

5. "You're LA-only. Can you support my territory?"

Distributors need brands that show up for in-salon training, events, issue resolution.
Counter: Made in LA, domestic fulfillment, 4-week lead time. Virtual training for distant markets + in-person for key launches. $5,100 Starter Package is a low-risk test. Distributor is the local expert - AWARE provides product + content.

6. "No brand awareness in my market."

Unlike Olaplex or K18, nobody outside SoCal has heard of AWARE.
Counter: 18M+ organic views are national. AWARE's Scalp Environment Report content generates inbound interest. Sample kits (1 per 6 retail units) so reps lead with product experience, not brand pitch.

7. "What if the founder loses interest or brand folds?"

Small founder-led brands are a real risk. Distributors have been burned before.
Counter: $125K revenue in year one, 96% repurchase, raising capital ($1-3M), patent-pending, GMP/MoCRA compliant. Offer to share sell-through data transparently - openness builds trust.

8. "We already carry Zenagen / K18 / Nioxin for scalp."

They think scalp care is already covered. Why add another brand in the same category?
Counter: AWARE is positioned differently - proactive scalp wellness vs. reactive treatment. Zenagen = hair loss, Nioxin = thinning, K18 = bond repair. AWARE is the "before there's a problem" product. Complementary, not competitive. Salons carrying both a treatment line and a wellness line see 40%+ higher scalp category revenue because they capture clients at different points in the care journey.

9. "What's your MAP policy?"

Distributors have been burned by brands that undercut salon pricing online.
Counter: Strict MAP enforcement at MSRP. Professional-only channel with anti-diversion clause written into every agreement. No Amazon, no mass retail, no unauthorized sellers - ever. We actively monitor and enforce. This is a non-negotiable term. Reference the Deal Terms section for full policy details.

10. "We need territorial exclusivity."

Distributors want to protect their investment in building the brand regionally.
Counter: Default is non-exclusive because we need multiple partners for national coverage. However, we're open to 6-month exclusivity with performance minimums (documented in Deal Terms floor). Exclusivity is earned through volume - hit agreed quarterly targets and we'll formalize it. This protects both sides.

11. "What marketing support do you provide?"

Distributors need to know the brand will invest in pull-through, not just push inventory.
Counter: Co-op fund (3-5% of wholesale), free samples kit for first 10-20 salons, co-branded education events, David conducts first 3 salon trainings in your territory, Scalp Bar activation support for top salons, social content library, and our Scalp Environment Report lead-gen tool that generates inbound client interest at the salon level.

12. "Your margins aren't competitive enough."

Distributors comparing to other brands' cost structures may push for deeper discounts.
Counter: Walk through the math. At target pricing (57% of wholesale), distributor cost is $15.68 per shampoo. Distributor sells to salon at $27.50 (50% of $55 MSRP) - that's a $11.83 margin per unit. With 96% repurchase every 6-8 weeks, that's recurring revenue. Compare to typical professional distributor margins of 20-35% - AWARE's 30-50% distributor margin is at or above market for premium lines.

Meeting Prep

What to Bring

  • Physical samples: shampoo, conditioner, leave-in
  • Discovery Kit (tangible hand-off)
  • Distributor line sheet (2-page)
  • Margin calculator visual (20-30% margin)
  • 1-page sell-through data (repurchase rate, reorder frequency)
  • Phone with social content clips (18M+ views proof)
  • Business cards

Open with Curiosity

  • "What are you hearing from salons about scalp health as a category?"
  • "Have any of your brands started moving into scalp-specific products?"
  • "How are your salons responding to the head spa / scalp care trend?"

Share the Insight (2-3 min max)

  • Scalp diagnosis + treatment + retail converging at the salon chair
  • AI making scalp personalization possible without a dermatologist
  • Salons doing scalp services seeing lift in color and styling spend
  • Consumer awareness outpacing salon readiness (18M+ views)

When They Push Back

"$55 is really high."50% salon margin at $27.50 wholesale is higher than most lines. 96% repurchase tells us price isn't a barrier once clients try it. Happy to send a sample kit.
"We just took on new brands."This isn't another brand - it's a category addition. Doesn't compete with color or styling. No rush - I'd love to stay in touch.
"50+ salons isn't much."You're right, we're early. That's why we want a distributor partner. 96% of those 50+ reorder every 6-8 weeks. Product-market fit is there.
"How do I know you'll be around?"$125K year one, raising capital, GMP certified, MoCRA compliant, patent-pending. Happy to share sell-through data openly.
"My reps won't know how to sell this."3 SKUs, not 20. Sell story: "your clients are searching for scalp care - here's what to offer." We provide training + sample kits. 18M views means clients walk in with awareness.
"What's your minimum?"$5,100 Starter Package. Enough to seed ~6 salons. Low risk to test the market.

What NOT to Do

  • Don't lead with pitch deck or line sheet - listen first
  • Don't oversell the scale (50 salons is 50 salons - own it)
  • Don't bad-mouth Zenagen, K18 - position as category expansion
  • Don't pressure for commitment - goal is a second conversation
  • Don't send margin calculator before meeting - it's a leave-behind

Success Criteria for Meeting #1

  • They agreed to receive samples
  • Follow-up date on calendar
  • You understand their brand evaluation process
  • You know who else is involved in the decision
  • They see scalp care as a category worth watching

The meeting is NOT about closing a deal. It's about starting a relationship.

  • Reviewed their full brand portfolio on their website
  • Identified which of their brands are scalp-adjacent
  • Found their new brand submission process
  • Checked their Instagram for recent posts/engagement
  • Identified 2-3 of their salon accounts in our area

  • Reviewed any red flags from the profile

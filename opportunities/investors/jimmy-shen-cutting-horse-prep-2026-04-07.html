Strategy

Talking Points for Jade

Lead With

  • Founder-market fit: Tina's 40+ years as a stylist and trichologist. No other scalp brand has this depth of professional credibility.
  • The white space: No dominant professional-exclusive scalp care brand exists. Nioxin is aging, Kerastase is a line extension, Olaplex doesn't address scalp. The category is wide open.
  • Channel as moat: Building in salons first means earning trust before scaling. This is capital-efficient and creates a distribution asset.

Let Him Discover

  • Organic growth with no paid acquisition
  • B2B repurchase behavior (signal product-market fit)
  • Distribution momentum (C.O. Bigelow launch, distributor conversations)
  • The education-first model (stylists as scalp consultants)

Don't Over-Share

  • Exact revenue numbers - save for a follow-up if interest is real
  • Full financial model - premature for a first call
  • Detailed use-of-funds breakdown - keep it high-level unless asked

Questions

What to Ask Jimmy

"What drew you to beauty investing originally, and how has your thesis evolved since Main Post?"
Gets him talking about what he looks for. Signals you did your homework without being heavy-handed about it.
"What does operator-led support actually look like at Cutting Horse? Where do Michael and Chris get most involved?"
Tests whether this is real value-add or just marketing. Wystrach built Freshly from scratch - his operational input could be genuinely useful for supply chain and DTC scaling.
"You mentioned the skinification trend - are you seeing other professional-channel beauty brands, or is most deal flow DTC?"
Maps their pipeline and reveals whether they have conviction about the professional channel or are defaulting to DTC assumptions.
"What stage does a company need to be at for Cutting Horse to lean in? What does a compelling first conversation look like for you?"
Directly calibrates expectations. He said $1-20M revenue - this tells you if AWARE is early, right-fit, or too nascent for them right now.

Anticipate

Questions He'll Likely Ask

"How big is the scalp care market?"
Global scalp care ~$18B and growing faster than any other hair segment. Professional channel is underserved - most growth is DTC/mass, which leaves the pro channel wide open.
"Why not go DTC first? Wouldn't that be faster?"
B2B is capital-efficient (near-zero CAC), builds credibility, and creates a professional endorsement moat. The plan is always to layer DTC on top of a professional foundation, not lead with it.
"What does your distribution strategy look like at scale?"
Direct salon relationships now, distributor partnerships in progress, selective premium retail (C.O. Bigelow is live). Never mass retail - salon-exclusive positioning is the strategy.
"Are you raising?"
Keep it open. "We're always interested in talking to the right partners. We're focused on building traction and would consider the right capital relationship at the right time." Don't name a number on a first call.

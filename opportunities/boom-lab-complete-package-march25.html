Part 2

Amendment to Manufacturing Agreement

AWARE Hair LLC and Boom Lab Inc · March 25, 2026

This Amendment ("Amendment") to the Manufacturing Agreement dated July 22, 2024 ("Original Agreement") is entered into as of March 25, 2026, by and between:

AWARE Hair LLC, a company organized and existing under the laws of the State of California, with its principal office located at 153 E Holly Street, Pasadena, CA 91103 ("Company"),

and

Boom Lab Inc, a company organized and existing under the laws of the State of California, with its principal office located at 6460 Coyote St, Chino Hills, CA 91709, California Corporation Number C4285797 ("Manufacturer").

Recitals

WHEREAS, Company and Manufacturer entered into the Original Agreement for the manufacture, filling, and packaging of hair care products;

WHEREAS, Manufacturer has proposed revised per-unit pricing to reflect increased material and production costs;

WHEREAS, Company has agreed to accept revised pricing and to assume responsibility for fragrance costs, subject to the terms set forth herein;

WHEREAS, Company has made total payments of $43,890.00 under the Original Agreement and its October 2024 Addendum;

WHEREAS, Manufacturer has delivered 7,400 of the originally contracted 30,000 retail units, with 400 shampoo units from Production Run 1 remaining undelivered;

WHEREAS, the initial one-year term of the Original Agreement expired on or about July 22, 2025, and both parties have continued to operate under its terms in good faith;

WHEREAS, the parties wish to renew the Original Agreement, formalize revised terms for future production, and preserve the Company's accrued credits;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants set forth herein, the parties agree as follows:

Article 1

Scope of Production

1.1

This Amendment covers the production of a minimum of 12,000 new retail units across the following products:

  • AWARE 9.1 Professional Shampoo 300ML
  • AWARE 9.1 Professional Conditioner 300ML
  • AWARE 9.1 Professional Leave-In Styling Conditioner 250ML
1.2

Flexible SKU Allocation. The allocation of units across SKUs shall be at Company's discretion, as specified in each Purchase Order (see Article 7). Each production run shall consist of 3,000 units of any SKU or combination of SKUs, at Company's choice. The total commitment of 12,000 units may be distributed in any combination across the three products, provided that the aggregate quantity meets or exceeds 12,000 units.

1.3

All units shall be produced and delivered by March 25, 2027.

1.4

Minimum Order Quantity. The minimum order quantity per SKU per production run is 3,000 units.

1.5

Production shall be organized in 4 runs of 3,000 units each, scheduled per mutually agreed Purchase Orders (see Article 7). Each Purchase Order shall specify the SKU mix and quantities for that run.

1.6

Supersession of Original Volume Commitment. This Amendment supersedes the original production volume commitment of 30,000 retail units under the Original Agreement. The parties' obligations with respect to future production quantities are solely as set forth in this Amendment. Neither party shall have any further obligation to produce, order, or accept units beyond the scope defined herein, except as may be agreed in a subsequent written amendment.

Article 2

Pricing

2.1

The per-unit prices for Products under this Amendment shall be:

Product Price Per Unit
AWARE 9.1 Professional Shampoo 300ML $3.85
AWARE 9.1 Professional Conditioner 300ML $3.25
AWARE 9.1 Professional Leave-In Styling Conditioner 250ML $3.65
2.2

These prices supersede the pricing in Section 5.1 of the Original Agreement for all production under this Amendment.

2.3

Total production value under this Amendment shall be determined by the actual SKU mix ordered. At an equal distribution (4,000 per SKU), the estimated total is approximately $43,000.00. The range based on SKU mix is $39,000.00 (all Conditioner) to $46,200.00 (all Shampoo).

2.4

Prices are fixed for the duration of this Amendment. Any further adjustments require a new written amendment per Section 13.4 of the Original Agreement.

Article 3

Ingredients and Formulation

3.1

Company-Supplied Ingredients. Notwithstanding Section 2.3 of the Original Agreement, Company shall source, pay for, and supply the following ingredients directly to Manufacturer for all production runs under this Amendment:

  • Fragrance: OnScent Pacific Air NF (or equivalent approved natural fragrance)
  • SO-Resisthyal-25 (hyaluronic acid complex)
  • SymHair Force 1631 (hair strengthening peptide)

Company shall deliver the above ingredients to Manufacturer in sufficient quantities prior to the start of each production run. The costs of Company-supplied ingredients are borne entirely by Company and are not reflected in the per-unit pricing set forth in Article 2.

3.2

Required Active Ingredients. All formulations produced under this Amendment shall include the following active and functional ingredients at the specified concentrations:

Ingredient Concentration Supplied By
SO-Resisthyal-25 1% of batch volume Company
SymHair Force 1631 1% of batch volume Company
OnScent Pacific Air NF (fragrance) 1% of batch volume Company
Camellia Sinensis (green tea) leaf extract Per formulation specification Manufacturer
Curcuma Longa (turmeric) root extract Per formulation specification Manufacturer
Beta Vulgaris (beetroot) root extract Per formulation specification Manufacturer

Concentrations listed as "1% of batch volume" are fixed unless otherwise agreed in writing by both parties. Concentrations listed as "per formulation specification" shall be as defined in the product specification sheet provided by Company per Article 8.

3.3

Formulation Integrity. Manufacturer shall not omit, substitute, or alter the concentration of any required active ingredient listed in Section 3.2 without prior written approval from Company. Any batch found to be missing a required ingredient or outside the specified concentration shall be considered non-conforming per Article 8.4.

3.4

Other Materials. Manufacturer remains responsible for procuring all other materials and ingredients per Section 2.3 of the Original Agreement, including but not limited to base surfactants, conditioning agents, preservatives, solubilizers, and packaging materials.

3.5

Storage and Accountability. Manufacturer shall properly store and maintain all Company-supplied ingredients per the specifications in Sections 1.2 and 3.2 of the Original Agreement, and shall account for all Company-supplied materials, reporting quantities used and any remaining inventory after each production run.

Article 4

Credit Recognition and Payment

4.1

Credit Balance. The parties acknowledge that Company holds a total credit balance of $43,890.00, calculated as follows:

Credit Item Date Amount
Initial deposit (30% of Original Agreement) July 2024 $27,720.00
Accelerated payment per October 2024 Addendum (pre-production) October 2024 $8,085.00
Accelerated payment per October 2024 Addendum (post-delivery) Early 2025 $8,085.00
Total Credit $43,890.00
4.2

Value of Goods Received. Company has received 7,400 units under the Original Agreement, valued at original contract prices:

SKU Units Received Original Price Value
Shampoo 300ML 2,100 $3.26 $6,846.00
Conditioner 300ML 2,500 $2.76 $6,900.00
Leave-In 250ML 2,800 $3.22 $9,016.00
Total 7,400 $22,762.00
4.3

Net Credit. The net credit balance to be applied to production under this Amendment is:

$43,890.00 − $22,762.00 = $21,128.00
4.4

Application of Credit. The net credit of $21,128.00 shall be applied evenly across the 4 production run invoices as follows:

  • Run 2 invoice: credit of $5,282.00
  • Run 3 invoice: credit of $5,282.00
  • Run 4 invoice: credit of $5,282.00
  • Run 5 invoice: credit of $5,282.00

Each run's production cost will be determined by the SKU mix specified in the Purchase Order, with the credit deducted from the invoice total.

4.5

Payment Terms for Remaining Balance. After application of the credit, the remaining balance for each production run shall be paid as follows:

  • 50% due upon submission of Purchase Order (before production commences)
  • 50% due upon delivery and acceptance of finished goods per Section 4.5 of the Original Agreement (Company shall have 5 business days to inspect and accept or reject)
4.6

Lab Fee Credit. For clarity, the $3,500.00 lab fee credit under Section 5.3 of the Original Agreement remains available for future product development and formulation work, and is not applied to production costs under this Amendment.

Article 5

Outstanding Obligations

5.1

Manufacturer acknowledges that 400 units of AWARE 9.1 Professional Shampoo 300ML from Production Run 1 remain undelivered (2,500 ordered, 2,100 received). These units were included in prior payments.

5.2

The 400 outstanding shampoo units shall be manufactured and delivered alongside the next production run under this Amendment, at no additional charge to Company.

5.3

Manufacturer may fulfill the 400 units in retail-sized (300ML) or bulk gallon format, at Company's election.

Article 6

Production Schedule

6.1

Manufacturer shall deliver completed production runs by the following dates:

Run Target Date SKUs Quantity
Run 2 Q2 2026 Per Purchase Order 3,000 units + 400 shampoo owed
Run 3 Q3 2026 Per Purchase Order 3,000 units
Run 4 Q4 2026 Per Purchase Order 3,000 units
Run 5 Q1 2027 (by March 25) Per Purchase Order 3,000 units
6.2

Exact quantities and SKU mix per run shall be specified in Purchase Orders per Article 7.

6.3

Manufacturer shall provide a minimum of 30 days notice if any production milestone is at risk of delay.

6.4

Lead time for each production run shall not exceed 45 days from receipt of deposit and Purchase Order.

6.5

Late Delivery. The late delivery penalties set forth in Section 4.6 of the Original Agreement (1% of invoice value per day of delay, up to 50% of total invoice value) shall apply to deliveries under this Amendment.

Article 7

Purchase Orders

7.1

Company shall submit a written Purchase Order for each production run, specifying:

  • SKUs and quantities
  • Per-unit pricing (per Article 2)
  • Requested delivery date
  • Delivery location
  • Payment schedule
7.2

Manufacturer shall confirm acceptance of each Purchase Order within 7 business days.

7.3

No production shall commence without a signed Purchase Order.

Article 8

Quality and Specifications

8.1

All quality standards, specifications, manufacturing processes, and compliance requirements set forth in Sections 1.2, 2.1 through 2.9, and 3.1 through 3.11 of the Original Agreement remain in full force and effect.

8.2

Specification Sheet. Company shall provide a product specification sheet for each SKU prior to each production run. The specification sheet shall define the required parameters including, but not limited to, pH range, viscosity, color, scent profile, active ingredient concentrations, and any other quality criteria. Manufacturer's quality control process shall verify that each batch conforms to the Company-provided specification sheet before filling and packaging.

8.3

Manufacturer shall provide Certificates of Analysis (COA) and batch records for each production run, demonstrating conformance to the specification sheet.

8.4

Any batch that does not meet the specifications defined in the Company-provided specification sheet shall be rejected. Manufacturer shall reformulate and reproduce the batch at Manufacturer's expense until specifications are met.

Article 9

Term and Renewal

9.1

The parties acknowledge that the initial one-year term of the Original Agreement expired on or about July 22, 2025, and that both parties have continued to operate under its terms in good faith since that date.

9.2

The Original Agreement is hereby renewed and extended through March 25, 2027, or until all production under this Amendment is complete and all outstanding obligations are fulfilled, whichever is later.

9.3

Further renewals beyond the term set forth in Section 9.2 shall be by mutual written agreement per Section 6.1 of the Original Agreement.

Article 10

General Provisions

10.1

Except as expressly modified by this Amendment, all terms and conditions of the Original Agreement and the October 2024 Addendum remain in full force and effect.

10.2

In the event of any conflict between this Amendment and the Original Agreement, this Amendment shall control.

10.3

This Amendment may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original.

10.4

This Amendment constitutes the entire agreement between the parties with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, and agreements relating thereto.

10.5

Governing Law and Disputes. This Amendment shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of California per Section 13.1 of the Original Agreement. Any disputes arising out of this Amendment shall be resolved per Section 13.2 of the Original Agreement (California state and federal courts).

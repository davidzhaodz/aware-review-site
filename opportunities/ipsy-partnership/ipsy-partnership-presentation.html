The Deal
IPSY includes AWARE Hair's 10ml leave-in conditioner sample in their Glam Bag, shipped to 360,000 subscribers. IPSY pays $0.39/unit ($140,400 gross). Payment is Net 60 after delivery, meaning cash arrives around August 18, 2026. AWARE handles all manufacturing, QC, and logistics.
The Opportunity

What is IPSY Glam Bag?

One of the largest beauty subscription services in North America. Curated sample-size products delivered to millions of subscribers monthly. Brands pay for manufacturing; IPSY provides the audience. A proven discovery channel for beauty brands at scale.

Brand Exposure

360,000 potential new customers will physically hold and try an AWARE product. This trial-driving exposure would cost $50K-$100K+ in paid digital advertising, with far lower conversion rates than a physical product experience.

Strategic Fit

Premium brands like Oribe and Bumble and bumble have used subscription box sampling without damaging their salon positioning. Key: drive IPSY recipients to DTC purchase - with salons concentrated in LA, DTC captures the national audience IPSY delivers.

CTA Strategy: Every sample should include a card with a QR code directing recipients to AWARE's DTC site. Goal: convert IPSY trial into full-size DTC customers ($110 AOV, ~80% margin). Salon partners remain the premium tier, while DTC captures the national IPSY audience beyond LA.

Contract Deep Dive - High Risk Clauses
1. Payment Terms (Net 60) Medium

IPSY pays 60 days after delivery window close or warehouse receipt, whichever is later. Payment ~August 18. Labeled "Co-Op Marketing Fees" not product purchase.

Negotiation
Push for Net 30 - moves payment to mid-July, reducing cash gap by $140K for 30 days.
2. Late Delivery Penalty High

2% per business day late, capped at 20%. Five days late = $14,040 (exceeds expected profit). Ten days late = $28,080.

Negotiation
Ask for 5-day grace period and reduction to 1%/day with 10% cap.
3. QC Penalty High

Flat 10% penalty ($14,040) for ANY non-conforming units. Binary - no proportional scale. "Non-conforming" not defined. Whether 10 or 10,000 units fail, same penalty.

Negotiation
Clarify definition + push for proportional penalty (only applies if >5% of units fail).
6. Indemnification High

Uncapped liability for ALL product claims + cross-brand indemnification (other brands in same Glam Bag). One lawsuit could exceed total deal value.

Assessment
Standard for industry but significant. Confirm insurance coverage with broker.
Contract Deep Dive - Medium & Low Risk Clauses
4. ExclusivityLow

6 months before + after: no competing subscription boxes (US/CA/MX). Does NOT affect salon, DTC, or retail. Acceptable as-is.

5. InsuranceResolved

$1M/$2M CGL + $5M umbrella. A.M. Best A- rating. Already covered - just add IPSY as additional insured ($500-$1,500).

7. IP LicenseMedium

Non-exclusive, worldwide. Survives termination for published content only. Cannot create new content post-termination. Standard.

8. CancellationMedium

90 days' notice for convenience. Anytime for cause. No reimbursement if costs already incurred. Request kill fee.

9. Right of OffsetMedium

IPSY can withhold payments against claimed liabilities. Broad "reasonable determination" language. Document everything meticulously.

10. Recall CostsHigh

100% AWARE - including consumer refunds, replacements, shipping, admin. Standard but significant at 360K scale. Confirm insurance covers recalls.

11. ReturnsMedium

Undistributed product returned for refund. AWARE pays shipping. Low likelihood - IPSY uses what they order.

12-14. OtherLow

Confidentiality: 3yr post-term, trade secrets perpetual. Law: CA, AAA arbitration SF (favorable). Termination: Material breach with cure period.

Negotiation Strategy

Ranked by impact. Even securing one or two of these significantly improves the deal.

Net 30 Instead of Net 60Priority 1

Biggest impact. Moves payment from ~Aug 18 to ~Jul 19. Reduces cash gap by $140K for 30 days.

Ask

"Given significant upfront capital, we'd like Net 30 to manage production cash flow."

Fallback

Milestone payment (30% at PPS approval, 70% Net 30 after delivery). Or Net 45 compromise.

Higher Unit Price ($0.55-$0.70)Priority 2

All-in cost is $0.36-$0.68/unit. At $0.55: revenue = $198,000 (reduces expected loss significantly). At $0.68: revenue = $244,800 (approximately breakeven at high COGS).

Ask

"All-in cost is $0.47-$0.68/unit. $0.68 is breakeven. We need $0.55 minimum to make this viable as a brand investment."

Fallback

Accept $0.55 minimum. Below that, losses exceed what brand exposure can justify.

QC Penalty ClarificationPriority 3

Current: binary 10% for ANY non-conforming units. Request proportional penalty (>5% failure threshold) + clear definition of "non-conforming." Exclude minor cosmetic issues.

Late Penalty Grace PeriodPriority 4

Ask: 5-day grace period + 1%/day with 10% cap. Fallback: accept 2%/day with 3-day grace and 10% cap (vs 20%).

Recommendation

Option A - Proceed As-Is

Expected loss: ~$48K (-34% margin). Best case is slightly profitable at low COGS. Requires $81K-$196K bridge. However, DTC upside at $110 AOV could offset losses significantly.

Option B - Negotiate Then Proceed

Push for Net 30, higher price, QC clarification, penalty reduction. Even one win significantly improves the deal. Net 30 alone solves the biggest cash flow concern.

Recommended

Option C - Decline

Valid if: cash flow can't be bridged, IPSY refuses all negotiation, ingredient Out List conflict, or manufacturer can't commit.

The three questions that determine this deal: 1) Can we bridge the $81K-$196K cash gap for 3-5 months? 2) Can we absorb uncapped indemnification risk on 360K units? 3) Does the DTC upside ($158K-$634K gross profit at $110 AOV) justify the deal risk?

Walk-Away Conditions: Cash gap unbridgeable. IPSY refuses ALL negotiation. Formula conflicts with Out List. Manufacturer can't commit to April-May window.

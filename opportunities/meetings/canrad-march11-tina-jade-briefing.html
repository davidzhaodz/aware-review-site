Canadian Regulatory (High Level)
Good News
Simple Classification
AWARE doesn't use zinc pyrithione, so all products classify as cosmetics (not drugs). This means a simple, free notification process with Health Canada.
Work Needed
Bilingual Labels
All product labels must include English and French. We'll need a full label redesign for the Canadian market (5-10 week process).
Important
Claims Language
Canada has strict rules about what you can say on product labels and marketing. See the claims table below.
What We Can and Can't Say in Canada
Safe to Say (Cosmetic Claims) Do Not Say (Triggers Drug Classification)
"Cleanses the scalp""Controls dandruff"
"Removes flakes""Treats scalp conditions"
"Soothes the scalp""Anti-fungal"
"Scalp-balancing formula""Regrows hair"
"Nourishes hair""Clinically proven to treat..."
"Hydrates and conditions""Therapeutic" / "Medicinal"
Why This Matters for Education
When Tina trains CanRad's reps or does in-salon education in Canada, the language needs to stay in the "cosmetic" lane. Focus on wellness, ritual, scalp health maintenance - not treatment of specific conditions. This is especially important for any printed or digital materials shared with salons.

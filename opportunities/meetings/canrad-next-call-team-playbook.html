Tina's Playbook
T
Tina
Founder, Trichologist, Product Authority

Your Superpower on This Call

  • You are the only trichologist on this call. That is rare and powerful. When you speak with clinical authority, it carries weight that nothing else can match.
  • Kenny is a business person - he responds to confidence and precision. The most impactful version of your story is the shortest one.
  • Think of it like a salon consultation: read the client, give them exactly what they need, no more.

Your Moment: After Kenny's Product Feedback (~90 seconds)

  • React to his specific experience - connect what he noticed to the science behind it. Whatever he mentions (texture, feel, scalp sensation), explain why that's happening from a formulation standpoint.
  • Drop the trichologist credential naturally - "In 40 years behind the chair and as a trichologist, I've tested everything. This formulation took 6 years because we refused to compromise."
  • One powerful line, then stop - "This is the product I put on my own daughter's scalp. That's the standard we hold it to."

If They Ask You a Direct Question

  • Answer it directly and specifically. One clear point. Then pause.
  • If it's a business/pricing question, redirect warmly: "David can speak to the specifics on that, but from my side..."

Power Moves

  • "As a trichologist..." (instant credibility)
  • Connect their experience to the science
  • One sentence about Jade's story, with emotion, then let it breathe
  • Short, confident statements that show expertise
  • Let silences work for you - they signal authority

Things to Save for Later

  • The full origin story (they heard it last time - they loved it)
  • Listing all the things the product does (pick the one most relevant to Kenny)
  • "I don't want to claim we're the best" (you don't need to hedge - the product speaks)
  • Global brand ambitions (too early for this audience)
  • Pricing or discount positioning (David's lane)

David's Playbook

Your Jobs on This Call

Handoff Signals - Smooth Transitions
How This Works
David moderates the call. When it's time for Tina or Jade to speak, David passes to them with a specific prompt (not an open-ended "do you want to add anything?"). When it's time to move on, David transitions to the next topic. This keeps the call moving and ensures everyone's contribution is focused.

Q1 The Distribution Journey
Say This
"I'd love to hear the full arc of the distribution side of hOURS. From when you first started thinking about getting into distributors to where you ended up - SalonCentric, Sweis, Nordstrom - what did that path actually look like? Where did it start?"
She tells the story
If she walks through the timeline (first distributor, then expanded):
"How long was it from the first distributor conversation to actually having product on shelves? And was there a tipping point where distribution started coming to you instead of you chasing it?"
She started DTC first
If she describes building DTC/salon base before distribution:
"That's interesting - so you proved the concept direct first. At what point did you feel like you were ready for distribution? Was there a revenue threshold or a signal that said 'now is the time'?"
She's high-level
If she gives the broad strokes without detail:
"That makes sense. What was the hardest part of the whole journey? Was it getting the first distributor to say yes, or was it something else entirely?"
Listen For
High-value signals: The order she pursued channels (which came first?), whether distributors approached her or she pursued them, how long each deal took, what she'd skip if she did it again. This question sets the tone. Let her talk. Don't interrupt with follow-ups too quickly - the best insights come when she's telling her own story.
Transition to Q2
"That's a great overview. Two Allure Best of Beauty wins is really hard to pull off - I'm curious how that happened and whether it changed the distribution conversations."

Don't lead with stats in the opening. Save for Phase 2 context-setting. Open with genuine curiosity about her story first.

Never share. This is confidential. Let her talk about margin structures first. Compare mentally, don't share.

Don't anchor first on price. Ask "What does your consulting model look like?" and let her name terms.

Here's what I'd love to do - I'd want to hire you for a focused session where we share our full distributor target list and the conversations we've got going, and you tell us what we're getting right and where we're off track. Think of it as a distribution strategy review with a written action plan as the deliverable. Would that be something you'd be interested in?"
If She Asks About Ongoing Work
"Right now we're focused on getting the strategy right. If this first session goes well and there's a natural fit for more, we'd be open to that. But let's start with the strategy review and see where it goes."
If She Offers Introductions
"That would be incredible - especially at Sweis. If introductions are part of what you'd include in the engagement, that makes the value even clearer. We'd absolutely want to compensate you fairly for that."
Decision Framework
Strong Go
Engage Immediately
Provides specific, actionable distribution insights beyond our research. Offers to make a specific introduction in the next 2 weeks. Has opinions that challenge or refine our thinking. Reasonable rate or open to blended compensation.
Go
Schedule Follow-Up
Good insights that add to what we already know. Has warm contacts she's willing to share. Names a reasonable consulting rate. Chemistry is good. Start with a single paid session.
Maybe
Single Paid Session First
Good insights but vague on introductions. Consulting rate is high but negotiable. Willing to do one paid strategy session before committing to ongoing. Worth testing the working relationship.
Pass
Thank and Move On
Generic advice that doesn't go beyond what we've already researched. No warm contacts to share. Rate is out of budget with no flexibility. Or steers toward her own agenda rather than our needs.
Meeting Success Criteria
The meeting is a success if AWARE walks away with:

1. At least 3 specific insights about distribution execution that we didn't have before (timeline, margin expectations, readiness criteria)
2. A clear understanding of whether she has warm contacts she's willing to share (and who they are)
3. Agreement on next step for a consulting engagement (even if it's "let me send you a proposal")
4. David's assessment of personal chemistry and whether this is someone he wants in AWARE's orbit long-term
5. Her candid opinion on whether $55 shampoo can survive distribution margins

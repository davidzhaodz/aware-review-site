Questions to Ask
Top 4 Must-Ask
1. "How many active DSCs are in the SBP network right now, and how many salon doors do they collectively reach?" - The whole model depends on this number.
2. "Do any of your current brands play in the scalp care or scalp treatment category?" - Tests portfolio gap for AWARE.
3. "What does a brand partner agreement look like? Walk us through the economics." - Margin structure determines viability.
4. "What's your team size, and what gets outsourced vs. handled in-house?" - Addresses the capacity concern directly.
Network Scale and Reach
# Question What We're Listening For
1 "How many active DSCs are selling in the SBP network right now?" Specific number. 10 DSCs = a startup. 50+ = a real network. "We're growing" = dodge.
2 "What's the geographic footprint? You mention both coasts - where specifically?" Western US primary is fine for a start. "Both coasts" was in Happi - verify if east coast warehouse is real and operational.
3 "What types of salons are your DSCs selling into? Independent, chain, barbershop, med spa?" Independent salons = our target. If it's mostly barbershops or beauty supply stores, positioning mismatch.
4 "What's the average monthly volume for a brand in your collective?" We need 200+ units/month to justify distributor margins. If brands are doing 50 units/month, the economics don't work.
Brand Partner Economics
# Question What We're Listening For
5 "Walk us through the brand partner economics. What does AWARE pay SBP, and what does a salon pay the DSC?" Standard distributor margins (brand nets 33-38% of MSRP) vs. different structure for the collective model. Lower overhead could mean better margins for brands.
6 "Is there minimum inventory commitment? What happens to unsold inventory?" Low/no minimums = aligned with the "no risk" positioning. High minimums = contradiction of his own pitch.
7 "Are brand partnerships exclusive or non-exclusive? Could we also work with Bassett or other distributors?" Non-exclusive = we can layer SBP alongside Bassett for broader reach. Exclusive = dealbreaker.
8 "What does your 3PL pricing look like? Is it per-unit, per-order, or bundled into the brand partner fee?" Transparent pricing = professional. "It depends" = potential hidden costs. Compare against our current fulfillment costs.
Operational Capacity
# Question What We're Listening For
9 "What's your team structure? How many people handle the 3PL, marketing, and education sides?" Specific roles and names = real operation. Vague = probably Noah + a few contractors. Not necessarily bad, but sets expectations.
10 "You mention 50+ brands. How many are actively selling through the network vs. listed but dormant?" Active brand count matters more than listed count. If 50 are listed but 10 are actively moved, that's a different story.
11 "How do your DSCs learn about new brands? What does the education/onboarding process look like?" Structured education program = brands get attention. "They have the catalog" = AWARE gets buried in a long list.
Positioning and Category Fit
# Question What We're Listening For
12 "Do any of your current brands play in scalp care or scalp treatment? Is there demand from salons for that category?" If yes, we fill a gap. If no, is there appetite? We'd be pioneering a category in their network.
13 "AWARE is positioned as premium, salon-exclusive, scientific-botanical. How does that fit alongside your current portfolio?" Does he see premium as aspirational for the network, or does he hesitate because his DSCs sell mid-market?
14 "You co-own My Salon Secret. How do you manage potential conflicts between your own brand and partner brands?" Same question we're asking Barry. Thoughtful answer = professional. Dismissive = concern.
The "Distribution is Sales" Philosophy
# Question What We're Listening For
15 "You said 'distribution is sales, not shipping.' What does that look like in practice for a new brand partner?" His philosophy is strong. Does the execution match? Specific sell-in strategies, DSC incentives, launch playbooks = real. Philosophy without process = vision without execution.
16 "What does your marketing support actually look like? Give us a specific example of what you've done for a brand." PR placement, social campaigns, event activation = real value. "We post on Facebook" = minimal. The Happi article is the only confirmed press for SBP itself.

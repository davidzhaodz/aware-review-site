Q&A Prep - Likely Questions

Market & Differentiation
What makes AWARE different from Oribe, Briogeo, or Act+Acre? Likely +
"Those are great brands, but they're lifestyle brands that added scalp care to their line. AWARE was built from the scalp up by a trichologist who's treated thousands of clients. Our patent-pending AwareBio Complex isn't a marketing angle - it's a clinically informed formulation targeting the scalp barrier specifically. And our salon-exclusive model means stylists, the people clients trust most with their hair, are our primary channel. We're not competing on shelf space at Sephora. We're embedded in the professional ecosystem."
TIP: Name-drop the patent-pending status early. It signals real IP, not just branding.
Why salon-exclusive? Isn't DTC where the growth is? Likely +
"We see salons as our wedge, not our ceiling. Think about how Olaplex started - professional channel first, built credibility, then expanded to retail. Salons give us something DTC can't: trusted recommendations. When a stylist tells a client their scalp needs help and hands them AWARE, the conversion rate is dramatically higher than any Instagram ad. We do have DTC as a replenishment channel, but the first touch happens through a professional. Our B2B repurchase rate validates this. Salons keep reordering because their clients keep asking for it."
TIP: The Olaplex comparison is powerful but don't overuse it. Mention once, then pivot to your own data.
How big is the scalp care market? Likely +
"The global scalp care market was valued at roughly $4 billion in 2024 and is projected to grow at 8-9% CAGR through 2030. But what's more interesting is the white space: professional-grade scalp care is virtually nonexistent. The existing market is dominated by anti-dandruff shampoos and basic treatments. We're carving out the premium, results-driven segment - similar to what happened in skincare 10 years ago when brands like Drunk Elephant elevated the category from drugstore basics to clinical efficacy."
TIP: Verify the market size number before the pitch. Use it as context, then quickly pivot to the white space narrative.
Traction & Proof Points
What's your revenue to date? Very Likely +
"We've done [PULL EXACT NUMBER FROM SHOPIFY] in revenue since launching in September 2025, with the majority coming through our B2B salon channel. What I'm most proud of isn't the top line - it's the quality of that revenue. Our B2B repurchase rate is 96%, meaning salons that carry us almost always reorder. That tells me the product works, the price point works, and the professional channel works."
TIP: Pull the exact number from Shopify before the pitch. Don't round too aggressively - specificity signals you know your business.
How many salons carry your products? Very Likely +
"We're in [EXACT NUMBER] salons across [REGIONS]. We've been deliberate about growth here - we're not trying to blanket the country. We focus on salons where the stylist genuinely understands scalp health and can educate their clients. That's why our retention is so high. We'd rather have 50 deeply engaged salon partners than 500 that just throw us on a shelf."
TIP: Quality over quantity narrative works well here. Frame selectivity as strategy, not limitation.
What does customer acquisition look like? What's your CAC? Likely +
"Our B2B acquisition is primarily through industry relationships, trade shows, and targeted outreach - so our CAC on the salon side is very low. On DTC, we're intentionally not spending on paid acquisition yet. Our strategy is to let the salon channel build brand awareness and credibility first, then layer in DTC marketing once we have a critical mass of professional advocates. We've seen this play out - clients who discover AWARE through their stylist have much higher lifetime value than cold DTC traffic."
TIP: Be honest about DTC being early. Frame it as strategic patience, not a gap.
Product & Science
Tell us about the AwareBio Complex - what's in it? Very Likely +
"The AwareBio Complex is our patent-pending formulation that targets the scalp barrier specifically. It combines [KEY INGREDIENTS - have Tina detail these] that work together to strengthen the scalp's natural defense system. What makes it different from other 'scalp care' products is that we're not just treating symptoms like flaking or oiliness - we're addressing the root cause, which is barrier dysfunction. Tina developed this based on years of treating clients in the chair, seeing what actually works versus what just sounds good on a label."
TIP: Have Tina lead this answer if possible. Her clinical credibility makes the science tangible.
Do you have clinical validation or studies? Possible +
"We have extensive real-world validation through our salon channel - hundreds of clients seeing measurable improvements in scalp health. We're currently in discussions about formal clinical studies to build that next layer of credibility. But I'd argue our 96% salon repurchase rate IS validation - professionals who stake their reputation on product recommendations don't keep reordering something that doesn't work."
TIP: If clinical studies are in progress, mention the timeline. If not, the repurchase rate IS your clinical proof - own it.
How does the gua sha fit into your product ecosystem? Possible +
"The sandalwood scalp gua sha is both a treatment tool and an entry point. It's our way of making scalp care a ritual, not just a product. When stylists use it during a service, it creates an experience that's memorable and shareable. It also differentiates us from every other brand selling bottles. The gua sha is paired with our leave-in treatment for a complete scalp ritual that clients can replicate at home, driving product repurchase."

Tough Questions (Rehearse These)
Why wouldn't a big player like L'Oreal or P&G just launch a scalp line and crush you? Hard +
"They probably will - and that's actually good for us. When big players enter a category, they validate it and expand the market. They'll do mass-market scalp care with big ad budgets. We don't need to compete there. Our moat is the professional channel and formulation credibility. L'Oreal can't replicate a trichologist founder, a patent-pending complex, or the trust that comes from a stylist recommendation. We're the craft brand in a market that's about to go mainstream."
TIP: Reframe competition as market validation. The craft beer analogy works: Budweiser making IPA didn't kill craft breweries, it grew the market.
Your revenue is modest. How do you plan to scale? Hard +
"We're 6 months in. At this stage, the quality of revenue matters more than the quantity. Every dollar we've earned came from a professional who chose to put our product on their shelf and recommend it to clients. That's not modest - that's a foundation. We're building the brand the way Olaplex did - one salon at a time until the professional community creates consumer demand. Our growth rate month-over-month is [X%], and we're just getting started with distributor partnerships that will accelerate coverage significantly."
TIP: Never apologize for your revenue. Reframe to growth rate and quality of revenue. Have the MoM growth percentage ready.
The salon channel is slow. How long until you're profitable at scale? Hard +
"We're already profitable on a per-unit and per-salon basis. The question is really about overhead and scale economics. Salon growth is steady but compounding - each new salon is a recurring revenue source with 96% retention. We're also adding distributor partnerships that accelerate coverage without proportional cost increases. The path to profitability at scale is more about reaching a salon count threshold than about changing the model."
What's your biggest risk right now? Hard +
"Execution speed. We have strong product-market fit and a clear strategy, but we're a small team. The risk is that the scalp care wave crests before we've built enough distribution to ride it. That's exactly why we're here - events like this, distributor conversations, and strategic partnerships are how we compress that timeline."
TIP: Showing self-awareness about risk builds credibility. Pick a real risk, then immediately explain how you're addressing it.

0:00 - 0:45 | THE HOOK
The Problem Nobody Talks About
Open with the gap: everyone focuses on hair, nobody focuses on the scalp. Yet scalp health determines hair health.
"Raise your hand if you've invested in a premium shampoo in the last year. Now - did you choose it for your hair, or for your scalp? That's the problem. We've been treating the symptom and ignoring the source. As a trichologist who's treated thousands of clients, I can tell you - most hair problems start at the scalp. And almost nobody in the beauty industry is addressing that with real science."
0:45 - 1:45 | THE SOLUTION
AWARE Hair + AwareBio Complex
Introduce the brand, the patent-pending complex, and the product line. Show (don't just tell) - bring products.
"AWARE Hair is salon-grade scalp wellness, built by a trichologist and stylist. Our patent-pending AwareBio Complex targets the scalp barrier - strengthening it, not just treating symptoms. The line includes a shampoo, conditioner, leave-in treatment, and our signature sandalwood scalp gua sha that turns scalp care into a ritual. Every product is formulated to work together as a system, not sold as individual SKUs."
1:45 - 2:45 | THE TRACTION
Proof It Works
Revenue, salon count, repurchase rate, testimonials. Numbers speak louder here.
"In 6 months since launch, we've generated [REVENUE] in revenue, built partnerships with [NUMBER] salons, and achieved a 96% B2B repurchase rate. Let me put that in context - salons are reordering because their clients are asking for it by name. We didn't spend a dollar on paid advertising to get here. Every sale came through professional recommendation."
2:45 - 3:45 | THE MARKET & MODEL
Why Now, Why This Way
Market size, B2B-first strategy, unit economics, scalability.
"Scalp care is a $4 billion market growing at 8-9% annually. But the professional-grade segment is wide open. Our strategy is B2B-first - like Olaplex's playbook. Salons build credibility, stylists create demand, and DTC becomes the replenishment channel. Margins are strong because we're not burning cash on paid acquisition or fighting for retail shelf space. We're building a brand the same way the best professional brands were built - from the chair up."
3:45 - 4:30 | THE TEAM
Why Us
Tina's trichology credentials, combined expertise, advisory.
"What makes AWARE different starts with who built it. Tina is a licensed trichologist and stylist who's spent [X] years treating scalp conditions. She didn't read about scalp health in a textbook - she sees it every day in the chair. I bring the business and technology side. Together, we've built a brand that has both clinical credibility and commercial traction."
4:30 - 5:00 | THE VISION
Where We're Going
Vision for the brand. End with something memorable.
"Our vision is simple: make scalp health as normal as skincare. In 3 years, we want AWARE to be the brand stylists reach for when a client asks about their scalp - in the same way they reach for Olaplex when someone asks about damage repair. We're building the category leader in professional scalp wellness, one salon at a time. And we're just getting started."

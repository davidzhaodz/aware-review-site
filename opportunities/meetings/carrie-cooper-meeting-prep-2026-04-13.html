Rep Assessment Questions

Each question tests a specific fit criterion. Use the "listen for" notes to evaluate her answers in real time.

Will She Prioritize AWARE or Will We Be Bottom of the Stack?

Priority & Bandwidth
"Walk me through a typical week - how do you split your time across your brands right now?"
How many brands she's actively repping. If it's 4-5, AWARE gets 1 day/week at best.
Priority & Bandwidth
"When two of your brands want you at the same account, how do you decide what to lead with?"
Does she lead with whoever pays most? Whoever's easiest? Or whoever fits the salon best? The honest answer tells you where AWARE falls.
Priority & Bandwidth
"If we started working together, what would you drop or deprioritize to make room?"
If the answer is "nothing, I can add it" - red flag. Time is zero-sum. If she names something specific, she's serious.

Can She Sell Early-Stage, or Only Established Brands?

Early-Stage Muscle
"What was it like selling SACHAJUAN in the US before it had name recognition here?"
Whether she's actually opened cold accounts for an unknown brand, or inherited existing distribution.
Early-Stage Muscle
"When a salon owner says 'I've never heard of this brand,' what do you do?"
Does she have a process? Samples, education, trial programs? Or does she rely on brand reputation to do the selling?
Early-Stage Muscle
"What's the smallest brand you've ever repped, and what was different about selling it?"
If every brand was already established, she may not have the muscle for AWARE's stage.

The SACHAJUAN Conflict

Brand Conflict
"SACHAJUAN has a scalp line at a similar price point. If a salon can only carry one scalp brand, how would you handle that?"
Honesty. "Oh they're totally different" = dodging. "That's a real question, here's how I'd think about it" = straight with you.
Brand Conflict
"Would you be comfortable not pitching SACHAJUAN's scalp products into accounts where you're representing AWARE?"
Whether she'll draw a clean line. If she hedges, the conflict is structural and won't go away.

Maria Nila Obligations

Legal / Non-Compete
"Do you have any non-compete or non-solicit from Maria Nila?"
A direct answer. If she doesn't know or says "I'd have to check," ask her to confirm before moving forward.
Legal / Non-Compete
"Are any of the SalonCentric or CosmoProf contacts you built at Maria Nila restricted?"
Whether she can actually use her best relationships or if they're locked up 12-18 months.

Does She Understand AWARE's Positioning?

Brand Alignment
"Our products are salon-exclusive - no Amazon, no Sephora, no mass retail. Some reps find that limiting. How do you feel about that?"
Does she see exclusivity as a selling point (scarcity, salon loyalty) or a handicap? If she immediately suggests retail expansion, wrong fit.
Brand Alignment
"We don't discount to close. If a salon pushes back on the $55 price point, how do you handle that?"
Whether she sells on value or on deal. Reps who default to "what if I get you 10% off" will erode your brand.

Actual Sales Mechanics & Volume

Territory & Volume
"How many salon accounts do you actively call on right now?"
A number. Vague answers ("a lot," "my network") mean she doesn't have a systematic territory.
Territory & Volume
"If you started repping AWARE next month, how many doors do you think you could open in 90 days?"
A realistic number with reasoning. 5-10 with a clear plan beats "50" with no specifics.
Territory & Volume
"What does your sales cycle look like? From first touch to first order, how long?"
Whether her process matches AWARE's (sample kit, education, close). If she's used to walk-in-and-write, she'll struggle with an education-led brand.

Economics (Without Tipping Your Hand)

Compensation Expectations
"What makes a brand worth repping for you financially? What's the math you run?"
Her minimum threshold. If she needs $3K/month guaranteed and you're offering commission-only, this won't work unless she believes in the volume.
Compensation Expectations
"You mentioned the stipend model with SACHAJUAN. Is that something you need from every brand, or does it depend?"
Whether commission-only is a dealbreaker. Don't offer 40% yet - let her tell you her floor first.

Reporting & Communication

Accountability
"How do you keep your brands updated on what's happening in the field?"
Does she have a CRM? Weekly reports? Or ad hoc? You need visibility into her activity if she's your only field presence.
Accountability
"If a salon has a product complaint or needs restock, how does that flow back to the brand?"
Whether she owns account service end-to-end or just opens doors and moves on.
Observe, don't ask: Don't ask "are you passionate about scalp care?" - it invites a performative answer. Instead, notice whether she brings up specific product details from trying the line. She received the products April 3. If she can name specific things about the formulas, texture, scent - she's engaged. If she just says "I loved them" with no specifics, the products aren't top of mind.

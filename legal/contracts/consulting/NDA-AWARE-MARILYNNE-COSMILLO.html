Legal Agreement
Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement
AWARE Hair LLC & MariLynne Cosmillo Consulting LLC
April 2026

This Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of ________________, 2026 ("Effective Date") by and between:

AWARE Hair LLC, a California limited liability company with its principal place of business at 153 E Holly Street, Pasadena, CA 91103 (hereinafter referred to as "AWARE" or "Company")

and

MariLynne Cosmillo Consulting LLC, with its principal place of business at _________________________________ (hereinafter referred to as "Consultant")

(each a "Party" and collectively the "Parties")

Recitals

Whereas, AWARE is the owner of certain proprietary business information, trade secrets, and confidential data relating to its professional scalp care product line, distribution strategy, financial performance, and salon partner relationships;

Whereas, Consultant possesses valuable industry experience, relationships, and knowledge related to professional beauty distribution, public relations, and brand growth strategy;

Whereas, the Parties wish to explore and engage in a consulting relationship relating to distribution strategy, brand growth, public relations, and related business advisory services (the "Purpose");

Whereas, in connection with the Purpose, each Party may disclose certain confidential and proprietary information to the other Party;

Now, therefore, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements set forth herein, the Parties agree as follows:

1. Confidential Information

1.1

Definition. "Confidential Information" means any non-public information disclosed by either Party to the other Party, whether orally, in writing, electronically, or by inspection, including but not limited to:

  • Business plans, strategies, financial data, revenue figures, cost of goods, pricing structures, margin analysis, and projections
  • Customer lists, salon partner information, distributor relationships, pipeline data, and sales data
  • Product formulations, manufacturing details, ingredient sourcing, and supplier relationships
  • Marketing strategies, PR plans, advertising campaigns, and unpublished creative materials
  • Trade secrets, know-how, processes, and proprietary methodologies
  • Terms of agreements with third parties, including distributors, manufacturers, and retail partners
  • Consultant's proprietary business methodologies, client lists, fee structures, and industry relationships
  • Any other information designated as "confidential" at the time of disclosure or that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential given the nature of the information and circumstances of disclosure
1.2

Exclusions. Confidential Information does not include information that the receiving Party can demonstrate by clear and convincing evidence:

  • Was publicly available at the time of disclosure through no fault of the receiving Party
  • Becomes publicly available after disclosure through no fault of the receiving Party
  • Was already known to the receiving Party prior to disclosure, as documented by written records
  • Is independently developed by the receiving Party without use of or reference to the disclosing Party's Confidential Information
  • Is rightfully received from a third party without restriction on disclosure
  • Is required to be disclosed by law or court order, provided the receiving Party gives the disclosing Party prompt written notice and cooperates in seeking a protective order

2. Obligations

2.1

Non-Disclosure. Each Party agrees to:

  • Hold the other Party's Confidential Information in strict confidence
  • Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without prior written consent of the disclosing Party
  • Use Confidential Information solely for the Purpose described in the Recitals
  • Limit access to Confidential Information to those individuals who need to know such information for the Purpose and who are bound by confidentiality obligations no less restrictive than those contained herein
  • Take reasonable measures to protect the confidentiality of the information, using at least the same degree of care used to protect its own confidential information of a similar nature, but in no event less than reasonable care
2.2

Client Relationship Confidentiality. Neither Party shall disclose the terms of the consulting relationship to any third party without the other Party's prior written consent, except as required by law. Notwithstanding the foregoing, Consultant may reference the engagement and display non-confidential, publicly available, or high-level descriptions of work performed for portfolio, educational, or marketing purposes, provided no Confidential Information, proprietary formulations, trade secrets, or unpublished data are disclosed.

3. Non-Compete Limitation

3.1

No Exclusivity. Nothing in this Agreement restricts the Consultant from providing consulting services to other companies in the beauty, haircare, or personal care industry, provided that such services do not involve the direct use or disclosure of AWARE's Confidential Information.

3.2

Conflict Notification. Consultant shall promptly notify AWARE if Consultant enters into an engagement with any company that directly competes with AWARE in the professional scalp care category, so that the Parties may determine whether a conflict of interest exists.

4. No Obligation

4.1

This Agreement does not obligate either Party to enter into any further agreement, business relationship, or consulting engagement. Neither Party is obligated to disclose any particular information to the other. This Agreement governs the handling of Confidential Information only and does not constitute a consulting agreement, employment agreement, or any other form of engagement.

5. Term and Termination

5.1

Term. This Agreement shall remain in effect for a period of two (2) years from the Effective Date.

5.2

Termination. Either Party may terminate this Agreement at any time by providing fifteen (15) days' written notice to the other Party.

5.3

Survival. The obligations of confidentiality shall survive termination of this Agreement for a period of two (2) years following termination or expiration.

6. Return of Information

6.1

Upon termination of this Agreement or upon written request, each Party shall promptly return or destroy all tangible and electronic materials containing the other Party's Confidential Information and certify in writing within fifteen (15) days that it has done so and that no copies have been retained in any form.

7. Remedies

7.1

Injunctive Relief. Each Party acknowledges that a breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable harm to the disclosing Party for which monetary damages would be inadequate. Accordingly, the disclosing Party shall be entitled to seek equitable relief, including injunction and specific performance, without posting bond, in addition to all other remedies available at law or in equity.

7.2

Attorney's Fees. The prevailing party in any action to enforce this Agreement shall be entitled to recover reasonable attorney's fees and costs.

8. General Provisions

8.1

Governing Law. This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of California, without regard to its conflict of laws provisions.

8.2

Jurisdiction. Any disputes arising under this Agreement shall be resolved in the state or federal courts located in Los Angeles County, California. The Parties consent to the exclusive jurisdiction and venue of such courts.

8.3

Assignment. Neither Party may assign this Agreement or any rights or obligations hereunder without the other Party's prior written consent. Any attempted assignment in violation of this Section shall be void.

8.4

Severability. If any provision of this Agreement is found invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect. The invalid provision shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it valid and enforceable while preserving the Parties' original intent.

8.5

Amendments. This Agreement may only be amended by a written agreement signed by both Parties.

8.6

Notices. All notices shall be in writing and sent to the addresses listed above or to such other addresses as may be specified in writing by either Party. Notices shall be deemed given when delivered personally, sent by confirmed email, or three (3) business days after being sent by certified mail, return receipt requested.

8.7

Waiver. No waiver of any provision shall be deemed a waiver of any other provision or subsequent breach. Any waiver must be in writing and signed by the waiving Party.

8.8

Counterparts. This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original, and all of which together shall constitute one and the same instrument. Electronic signatures shall be deemed equivalent to original signatures.

8.9

Entire Agreement. This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior and contemporaneous agreements, understandings, negotiations, and discussions, whether oral or written.

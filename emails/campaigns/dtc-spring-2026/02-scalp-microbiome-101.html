◆ From the Lab ◆

The Invisible Ecosystem on Your Scalp

There are more microorganisms living on your scalp right now than people on Earth. Understanding them is the key to understanding your hair.

What Is It

Your scalp microbiome is a community of bacteria, fungi, and other organisms that live on and in the skin of your scalp. When it's in balance, your scalp stays calm, hydrated, and naturally defended against irritation.

When it's disrupted - by harsh sulfates, over-washing, stress, or environmental shifts - the scalp loses its ability to self-regulate. That's when you see flaking, excess oil, sensitivity, and even hair thinning.

◆  Myth vs. Reality  ◆

Three Things Most People Get Wrong

"If I have dandruff, I need a stronger shampoo."

Often the opposite. Aggressive cleansers strip protective organisms along with the bad ones, making the underlying imbalance worse. A gentler, microbiome-friendly cleanser can actually resolve flaking faster.

"Clean scalp = healthy scalp."

"Squeaky clean" means you've stripped the scalp's natural oils and disrupted the microbiome. A healthy scalp feels comfortable and balanced between washes, not tight or dry right after.

"Scalp care and hair care are the same thing."

Hair is dead tissue. The scalp is living skin - the only part of the equation that you can actually improve. Healthy hair starts with a balanced scalp environment, not shinier strands.

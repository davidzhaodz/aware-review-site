Color Aftercare

Why Is My Scalp So Sensitive After Color?

It's not just the hair that needs recovery.

Color / Bleach + Scalp

◆ Scalp Insight ◆

Post-color care usually focuses on the hair. But from a trichologist's perspective, the scalp deserves just as much attention.

Color and bleach expose the scalp to alkalinity, oxidation, and prolonged chemical contact. Even when done correctly, the scalp can be left vulnerable for days afterward.

Smart aftercare isn't just about preserving the color result - it's about helping the scalp return to balance.

◆ Aftercare Guide ◆

5 Essentials After Color

Reduce unnecessary stress while the scalp recalibrates.

1

Cleanse gently, but don't skip it

Avoiding washes lets sweat and residue build up on an already sensitized scalp. Cleanse on schedule with a formula that removes buildup without stripping.

2

Skip the scrubs for now

Physical exfoliants, strong acids, and harsh anti-dandruff treatments can make a vulnerable scalp feel worse if used too soon after color.

3

Minimize friction

Use fingerpads instead of nails. Avoid tight ponytails, rough towel drying, and heavy brushing while the scalp is recovering.

4

Go easy on direct heat

Hot tools and blow dryers amplify that tight, reactive feeling. If the scalp feels tender, that's a sign to back off.

5

Watch for symptoms that persist

Mild sensitivity is normal. Persistent burning, swelling, or unusual shedding is not - consult your stylist.

◆ Lab Note ◆

Why pH Matters After Color

Healthy Scalp

4.5 - 5.5

Slightly acidic

After Bleach

9 - 11

Highly alkaline

A healthy scalp sits around pH 4.5 to 5.5. Bleach and oxidative color push that to pH 9-11 - temporarily disrupting the barrier that retains moisture and keeps skin stable.

Post-color care should help the scalp return to its naturally acidic state. A scalp that rebalances quickly stays calmer, more comfortable, and more resilient.

From Our Trichologist

For the first few washes after color, use the pads of your fingers and massage shampoo in slowly. No nails, no rough scrubbing.

A good rule of thumb: if your scalp feels tighter after washing than before, your routine is probably too aggressive.

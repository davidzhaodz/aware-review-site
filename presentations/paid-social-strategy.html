01

Philosophy

Restraint is the Strategy

Our unit economics don't support cold paid acquisition yet. So we're not doing cold acquisition. We're amplifying what already works - taking proven organic content to audiences who are already warm or adjacent.

Boosted organic content sees 60-80% lower CPMs than cold creative because the content is already validated by real engagement. Every dollar moves warm and adjacent audiences toward the Scalp Environment Report - our primary email capture mechanism. This builds an owned audience we control, not rented attention.

Competitive Context

KilgourMD, the closest DTC scalp care competitor, runs ~1,700 active ads across Meta and Google with estimated spend of $100K-500K+/month. They buy every customer through quiz funnels and long-form advertorials because they have no salon channel. That model requires VC funding and infinite CAC tolerance. Ours does not. Our salon partners, 96% reorder rate, and Scalp Environment Report generate warm audiences that paid media simply amplifies.

I

Boost, Don't Create

Only amplify posts with 2x average engagement. Prioritize educational and science-backed content - in scalp care, education IS the ad. Product-only posts underperform.

II

Owned Over Rented

Every ad drives to the Scalp Report for email capture. We're converting Meta's rented reach into our owned Klaviyo list.

III

Data Before Scale

This pilot generates the cost-per-lead and audience data needed to make an informed scale decision. No assumptions, only evidence.

03

Test Structure

30 Days, Three Phases

A disciplined elimination tournament. We test all three audiences in parallel, kill the weakest at Day 14, optimize the survivors, then consolidate into the proven winner for the final push.

Phase 1

Parallel Test

All three segments run simultaneously with equal creative. Day 3 delivery check, Day 7 first report, Day 14 decision: kill the worst performer.

Days 1 - 14

Phase 2

Optimization

Redistribute budget to top two (60/40 split). Introduce a second creative per surviving segment. Test three content angles: (a) educational science posts (microbiome, hard water, environmental factors), (b) salon/professional credibility posts, (c) founder or behind-the-scenes posts. Day 21 decision: consolidate if a clear leader emerges.

Days 15 - 21

Phase 3

Scale Winner

All budget concentrated on the winning segment and creative. Monitor frequency cap (max 3.0), swap creative if fatigued. Day 30: full month report and Month 2 budget decision.

Days 22 - 30

Test Isolation Protocol

Phase 1: Audience

Same creative, same budget. Only the audience segment varies.

Phase 2: Creative

Same audiences (top 2), adjusted budget. Creative type varies.

Phase 3: Scale

Same audience + creative (winner). Budget concentration varies.

Benchmark Context

Cold DTC scalp care brands (KilgourMD, Act+Acre) typically pay $15-40+ per email in cold acquisition. Our warm/retargeting audiences should achieve significantly lower CPL. If we're hitting $8-12 per email with warm audiences, something is wrong with the creative or landing page - not the channel.

Kill Criteria

If cost per email capture exceeds $15 after 14 days of spend across all segments, we pause everything. Reassess creative selection, audience targeting, and landing page conversion rate before restarting. Any ad exceeding 3.0 frequency is paused immediately regardless of performance. No exceptions, no "one more week."

Decision Framework

Month 2: Data Decides

No predetermined outcome. The 30-day data determines whether we scale, hold, reduce, or stop. Four scenarios, four clear actions.

< $5 per email

Scale to $750-1,000/month

Activate B2B Salon Awareness brief (Brief 2). Scale the winning DTC segment. We've found product-channel fit.

$5 - $10 per email

Maintain at $500/month

Refine targeting and creative within the winning segment. Test new content types. Promising but not yet proven.

$10 - $15 per email

Reduce to $300/month

Test different post types and audiences. Consider landing page optimization. The funnel has friction.

> $15 per email

Pause paid entirely

Focus on organic growth and Scalp Report SEO. Revisit paid in 60 days with better organic traction. No sunk cost thinking.

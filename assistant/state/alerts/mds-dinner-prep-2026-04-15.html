The Boycott - Full Story

What Amazon changed: Starting April 15, Amazon would eliminate credit cards as a payment method for ad spend. Instead, ad costs would auto-deduct from seller proceeds. Sellers currently run ads on credit cards to earn 2-4% cash back and preserve operating capital. Forced deduction means the money that should fund next month's inventory gets pulled for ads. Amazon simultaneously rolled out a fuel surcharge and increased fulfillment fees. verified

A poll of 100+ MDS founders found the majority would lose over $100K in available cash from the combined changes, and nearly 27% projected losses exceeding $250K.

Eugene's role: He organized the boycott from inside MDS, building the coalition to ~150 committed participants plus unaffiliated sellers. His stated goal: "This is going to show them that sellers can unite against these changes."

Amazon's response: On April 14 - yesterday, the day before the boycott - Amazon announced it was deferring the payment change until August 1, 2026. Their exact language: "Based on feedback we heard, we're deferring this change until August 1, 2026 to give this group of advertisers more time to prepare." verified

The August 1 question: The deferral bought four months. Does MDS plan to keep the coalition warm? This is a real strategic question he hasn't resolved yet.

The Eparé connection: His whole trajectory is about being burned by over-reliance on one channel. That's not abstract for him - it's personal history.

What $1M on Amazon actually looks like: After fees and advertising, a seller doing $1M nets $550-650K in product margin - before COGS, returns, and overhead. For commodity goods with 40-50% gross margins, the math barely works. For premium goods with 60-70% margins, it's viable but tight. verified

What's Making Sellers Angry

  • Jan 2026: FBA prep and labeling services eliminated - all units must arrive pre-labeled
  • The ad payment policy change (today's boycott)
  • 3.5% fuel surcharge on top of existing fulfillment fees (starts April 17)
  • Each year brings a new fee category with a new acronym
  • A poll of 100+ MDS founders: majority would lose $100K+ in available cash from combined changes

Amazon to TikTok Migration

TikTok Shop hit $33.8B global GMV in 2025, projected $50B in 2026. unverified

The honest reality: most Amazon sellers who try TikTok Shop fail within 90 days. The core mistake is treating it like "another Amazon channel." Your 10,000-review Amazon listing means nothing on TikTok if no one is making videos about it.

Where Amazon instincts go wrong on TikTok:

  • Reviews don't transfer - social proof is creator-driven, not review-driven
  • SEO instincts are wrong - discovery is algorithmic and content-driven
  • Polished content underperforms - studio photography loses to raw creator demos
  • Content velocity - Amazon listings sit and convert for years; TikTok rewards freshness
  • Returns run 5-8% higher - TikTok buyers are more impulse-driven

Strategic framing: TikTok creates cultural demand, Amazon captures it. Many sophisticated operators treat them as sequential - go viral on TikTok, harvest on Amazon. The platforms are complementary, not competitive.

FBT warning: As of Feb 25, 2026, TikTok ended independent seller shipping and pushed all US sellers into centralized FBT (Fulfilled by TikTok). Some sellers report fulfillment issues. verified

What's driving it: More advertisers competing on a platform with reduced targeting granularity (privacy restrictions gutted behavioral micro-targeting). Creative quality is now the primary differentiator - brands running 20+ new ad creatives monthly reportedly achieve 65% higher ROAS. verified

Is email/SMS a replacement? No - it's a retention tool, not an acquisition replacement. Email flows generate ~41% of total email revenue from just 5.3% of sends. Adding SMS to abandoned cart lifts recovery to 38%. But email doesn't solve the acquisition problem - it improves LTV on customers you already paid to acquire. The pitch that "email replaces paid social" is wrong. The accurate version: owned channels improve the economics of paid social by making each acquired customer worth more. verified

Tariffs

The Trump administration's tariff escalation raised the US weighted-average tariff from ~2% to over 20% since April 2025. China-origin goods face the highest exposure. The de minimis exemption ($800 duty-free threshold that powered Shein and Temu) ended August 29, 2025. Parcel volume under $800 entering the US fell 54% afterward. verified

Impact on DTC brands: Apparel, electronics, beauty, and home goods sourcing from China. Everlane publicly calculated an $11 cost increase per cashmere sweater at 25% tariffs. Brands with thin margins and no pricing power have no good options.

Responses: Mexico nearshoring is the most common pivot - labor costs 20-30% cheaper than China. Most operators are eating margin while scrambling to diversify. There is no clean solution.

Your positioning advantage

You manufacture domestically (Boom Lab). In a room where everyone is sweating tariffs on China-sourced goods, "we manufacture in the US" is a genuine competitive advantage worth mentioning casually - not as a pitch, but when tariffs come up naturally.

The Profitability Shift

The narrative has flipped completely from "growth at all costs" to "profitable from day one." Brands that raised venture capital to subsidize CAC in 2021-2022 are either dead or restructuring.

DTC benchmarks:

  • Gross margin: 50-65% average, beauty/personal care 60-70% verified
  • After marketing (20-35%), fulfillment, overhead: median net profit 3-10% unverified
  • Mid-market ($10-50M) "dead zone": ~7-8% EBITDA unverified

Why your B2B-first model is interesting to this crowd: The standard DTC path (pay $130+ CAC, hope LTV pays out over 18 months) is the consensus problem everyone is trying to solve. B2B gross margins are lower (40-50% wholesale vs. 60-70% DTC), but the economics are predictable, there is no CAC, and repurchase happens without ad spend. The trade-off is scale ceiling. Worth naming if it comes up - it runs counter to the instinct in this community.

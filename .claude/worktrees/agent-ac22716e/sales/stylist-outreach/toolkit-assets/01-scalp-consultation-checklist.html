Scalp Consultation Checklist

For Professional Stylists
Pre-Service Questions
"Has anything changed in your environment or routine recently?"
Moved, traveled, new water filter, pool/ocean exposure
"What's your water like at home - hard, soft, filtered?"
Hard water deposits minerals; soft water can over-condition
"How much sun exposure do you typically get?"
UV damages scalp skin; part lines most vulnerable
"Any scalp concerns - itching, flaking, tightness, oiliness?"
Note severity and when it started
"What products are you currently using at home?"
Include styling products, not just shampoo/conditioner
Visual Assessment
Overall scalp color
Pink (healthy), red (irritation), pale, uneven
Flaking or buildup present
Note location, size, color of flakes
Oiliness distribution
All over, crown only, near face, etc.
Hair density and distribution
Note any thinning areas or changes
Any bumps, irritation, or unusual areas
Document location; refer to dermatologist if concerning
Touch Assessment
Scalp mobility
Tight (tension/stress) vs. loose (normal)
Temperature
Warm areas may indicate inflammation
Texture
Smooth (healthy) vs. rough (dry/damaged)
Sensitivity when touched
Note if client reports discomfort
Environmental Factors to Note
Season/weather changes
Water quality in client's area
Air quality/pollution exposure
UV exposure frequency
Swimming (chlorine/salt water)
The 4-6 Week Lag
What you see in the hair today often started at the scalp 4-6 weeks ago. Current environmental exposure = visible effects next month.

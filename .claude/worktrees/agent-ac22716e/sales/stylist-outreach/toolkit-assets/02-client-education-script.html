Client Education Script

Word-for-Word What to Say About Scalp Health
How to Use This Script
These are conversation frameworks, not rigid scripts. Adapt the language to your natural voice. The goal is to educate clients about scalp health in a way that feels helpful, not salesy.
The Opening (During Consultation)
Before You Begin the Service
"Before we get started, I'd like to take a quick look at your scalp. A lot of what shows up in your hair actually starts at the scalp, so this helps me give you better recommendations."
Do your visual and touch assessment. This takes 30-60 seconds.
If You Find Something
The Discovery
"I'm noticing [observation] on your scalp. This is pretty common, and it often connects to [environmental factor]. Have you noticed any changes in your [hair/scalp] lately?"
Pause and listen. Let them share their experience.
The Explanation
"What I'm seeing might be related to [cause]. The good news is that scalp health is something we can work on together."
Keep it positive - frame as opportunity, not problem.
Common Observations & How to Explain Them
Dryness / Tightness
"Your scalp is showing some dryness, which can happen from hard water, over-washing, or environmental stress. The scalp is skin, so it responds to the same things your face would."
Flaking
"I'm seeing some flaking here. This could be dryness, product buildup, or a response to something in your environment. Let's figure out what's going on."
Oiliness
"Your scalp is producing more oil than usual. Sometimes this happens when the scalp is actually dry - it overcompensates. Or it could be product-related."
Redness / Irritation
"There's some redness here, which usually means irritation or inflammation. Have you changed anything recently - products, water, stress levels?"
Transitioning to Recommendations
The Bridge
"Based on what I'm seeing, I'd recommend [specific action]. This will help [specific benefit]."
Recommendation Options:
- "Using a gentler cleanser and focusing on the scalp, not just the hair"
- "Adding a scalp treatment to your routine once a week"
- "Being mindful of [environmental factor] and protecting your scalp"
- "Coming in for a scalp treatment before your next color service"

If They're Skeptical
The Skincare Analogy
"I know it sounds like a lot to think about, but the scalp really is the foundation. Think of it like skincare for your head. When we address what's happening at the root - literally - your hair responds."
The Time Lag Explanation
"Here's something most people don't realize: what you see in your hair today actually started at the scalp about 4-6 weeks ago. So if we address scalp health now, you'll see the results in your hair over the next month or two."
The Close
Document and Follow Up
"I'll make a note of what we talked about today so we can track how things change. Next time you're in, we'll check in on your scalp again."
Document findings in their client file. This builds trust and shows professionalism.
Handling Common Questions
"Is this normal?"
"It's more common than you'd think. Our scalps respond to everything - stress, weather, water quality, products. The fact that we're catching it means we can address it before it affects your hair."
"Do I need to see a doctor?"
"What I'm seeing is something we can work on together through proper care. But if it doesn't improve in 4-6 weeks, or if you notice [specific symptoms], a dermatologist visit is a good idea."
"What products should I use?"
"Let me recommend a few things based on what I'm seeing. [Give specific recommendations.] The key is being consistent and giving it time - scalp health doesn't change overnight."
"How long until I see results?"
"You should notice your scalp feeling better within 2-3 weeks. The hair itself takes longer - about 4-6 weeks for changes at the scalp to show up in the hair."
Phrases to Avoid (and What to Say Instead)
"You have a problem" -> "I'm noticing..."
"You need to buy..." -> "I'd recommend trying..."
"This is bad" -> "This is common, and here's what we can do"
"You're doing it wrong" -> "There's an opportunity to adjust..."
Remember: You're the expert, but position yourself as a partner in their hair health journey. Education builds trust. Trust builds loyalty.

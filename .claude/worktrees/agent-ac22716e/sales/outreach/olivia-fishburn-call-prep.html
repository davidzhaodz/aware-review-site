5. The Call (~15 minutes, 3 blocks)

Minutes 0-3 · Warm Up

Apologize briefly, then get her talking

One sentence on the delay. Then go straight to asking about her experience - she wants to talk about it.

"Olivia, first off, so sorry about the delayed response - I switched email services at the worst time and missed your messages. But reading your feedback honestly made my week. How did everything hold up in Aruba?"
Listen for: Which products she gravitates toward. Whether she used them on clients or just herself. Any language about "my salons" or "my accounts" that signals how she talks about her territory.
Minutes 3-10 · Learn Her World

Understand how she works and what her salons need

This is the most important block. Don't pitch - ask questions and let her run. She's a field expert who sees 100+ salons regularly. Her perspective is worth more than any market research.

"You mentioned you're a fan of the Milbon head spa category. Are your salon owners actively asking for scalp services, or is that still something you have to educate them on?"

Follow-up questions (pick 1-2 based on flow, not all):

  • "What's the biggest thing your salon owners push back on when you bring them a new brand?"
  • "How does it usually work when SSG picks up a new line? Does it start with a few reps testing it, or is it more top-down?"
  • "If you were going to bring AWARE to a few of your accounts, which ones would you start with?"
Listen for: How SSG's brand eval works from the field level. Whether DSCs have input on new brands or if it's purely a McKenzie/Gino decision. How she'd position AWARE vs. existing brands like Zenagen. Whether she's already mentioned AWARE to anyone at SSG.
Minutes 10-15 · Next Steps

Handle reorder + let the SSG intro emerge naturally

She already offered the intro on March 12 and she's now a genuine convert. Don't hard-ask - she'll bring it up. If she doesn't by minute 12, gently reference it.

"Let me get you stocked up - I'll send you a full set. And I was thinking about what you mentioned when we first connected, about being open to making an intro at SSG. Now that you've actually used everything, does that still feel right to you?"

If she says yes:

  • "Who would be the best person to start with - McKenzie?"
  • "Would it help if I sent a sample kit directly to her too?"
  • "What would make this easiest for you to bring up internally?"

If she hedges:

  • "No pressure at all. Would it make sense for me to meet the team at Cosmoprof Vegas in July instead?"
  • "What if you tested with a couple of your accounts first, and we go from there?"
"This has been really helpful. I'll get your reorder shipped today. And [whatever she suggests for SSG]. Thanks again for being so generous with your time and your feedback."

6. Driving Call Rules

Do

  • Let her monologue - she's a storyteller and she's driving
  • Ask one question at a time, then wait
  • Reference her specific feedback ("the curly hair test," "Aruba")
  • Be a student: "How does SSG usually..."
  • Offer the reorder as a gift, not a transaction
  • If she mentions Zenagen, say "we'd be complementary to that"
  • End with ONE clear next step, confirmed verbally

Avoid

  • Don't rapid-fire questions (she's driving, not in a meeting)
  • Don't dump stats unprompted (96% repurchase, etc.)
  • Don't mention the McKenzie cold email campaign
  • Don't over-explain the email delay - one sentence, move on
  • Don't pitch SSG distribution - she's an advocate, not a buyer
  • Don't compare AWARE to Milbon or Zenagen negatively
  • Don't ask her to "sell" you in - let her volunteer how she'd do it
  • Don't bring up pricing/margins (that's a McKenzie conversation)

Seq 1: Scalp Wellness + Category

Primary signal: They already care about scalp/wellness - AWARE is a natural category addition.

Assign when ANY are true:
  • Salon lists scalp treatments, scalp detox, or head spa services
  • Carries wellness brands (Davines, Oway, Innersense, Aveda, Act+Acre)
  • Mentions "wellness," "holistic," "scalp health," "clean beauty"
  • Offers specialized treatments beyond basic cut/color
  • Positions as organic/clean/green beauty

Seq 2: Exclusivity + Credibility

Primary signal: They value professional exclusivity - AWARE protects their margin.

Assign when ANY are true:
  • Carries brands gone DTC/Amazon (Olaplex, K18, Briogeo)
  • Mentions "professional-only" or "salon-exclusive" positioning
  • Reviews indicate frustration with online product availability
  • Strong local reputation (4.5+ rating, 100+ reviews)
  • Attends trade shows (Cosmoprof, IBS, Premiere)

Seq 3: Education + Certification

Primary signal: They invest in education and personal brand - certification is the door opener.

Assign when ANY are true:
  • Stylists active on Instagram/TikTok (individual accounts)
  • Emphasizes education or continuing education in marketing
  • Younger/entrepreneurial owner (opened within last 5 years)
  • Team posts educational content (tutorials, tips, before/afters)
  • Smaller salon (1-3 chairs) where owner IS the primary stylist

Tiebreaker Rules

  1. Scalp wellness wins over exclusivity - they're already in the category
  2. Exclusivity wins over education - higher-intent signal
  3. Education is the default when signals are weak - lowest commitment ask

Fit Scoring

HIGH 4.5+ rating, 50+ reviews, premium pricing, scalp/wellness focus, active IG, professional lines
MED 4.0+ rating, 25+ reviews, mid-premium pricing, color/general services, some online presence
LOW 3.5+ rating, basic online presence, general services, location-based inclusion

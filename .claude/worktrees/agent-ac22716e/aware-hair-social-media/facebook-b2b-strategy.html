Three-Tier Strategy

Organic foundation first, paid when proven, AI tools as force multipliers throughout.

Tier 1: Organic Foundation ($0-34/month)

Group participation and Page content are $0. AI monitoring tools (Groups Watcher $19/mo, ManyChat $15/mo) are optional but recommended force multipliers that bring the total to $34/month.

2A. Facebook Group Participation Strategy

The highest-ROI organic play. Salon owners are already discussing products, sharing frustrations, and asking for recommendations in groups with tens of thousands of members.

Participation Protocol

  1. David joins with personal profile (transparent about AWARE connection)
  2. Weeks 1-2: Observe only. Map culture, rules, key influencers, recurring questions
  3. Week 3+: Begin contributing genuinely helpful business content (not product pitches)
  4. Helpful contributions: Answering questions about retail strategy, pricing, client retention; sharing industry data/trends; responding to product complaints with educational perspective; offering business insights from AWARE's experience
  5. Mention AWARE only when: Directly asked about professional scalp care lines; someone describes a problem AWARE specifically addresses; sharing honest business-building content
  6. Never: Copy-paste across groups; DM members unsolicited; make medical claims; pitch aggressively; fake grassroots participation
Engagement ratio: 10 genuinely helpful comments for every 1 that mentions AWARE. Consistently.

Content published in Facebook Groups gets more than twice the engagement compared to content posted on business pages. Verified Salon Guru. This makes organic Group participation a higher-leverage play than simply posting B2B content on the AWARE Page.

Attribution Model

B2B attribution is harder than DTC. Salon owners may see AWARE in a Group, visit the website weeks later, and then respond to a cold email. Attribution goes to the cold email, but Facebook created the awareness.

Multi-Touch Tracking System

  1. UTM parameters: All Facebook links tagged (utm_source=facebook, utm_medium=organic/paid, utm_campaign=b2b_[name])
  2. Notion pipeline source: Record ALL touchpoints (Facebook Group, ad, email, trade show)
  3. Post-conversion survey: After first wholesale order, ask: "Where did you first hear about AWARE?"
  4. Cross-reference Klaviyo: Check if salon lead was tagged facebook_b2b_lead before converting through another channel
  5. Meta Conversions API: Server-side tracking for offline conversion events (first wholesale order synced back to Meta)
Attribution rule: Credit the channel that generated the first touchpoint, track all subsequent touchpoints. This gives Facebook credit for awareness even when cold email closes the deal.

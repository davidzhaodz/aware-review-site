Facebook DTC Strategy for Affluent 60+ Women

Facebook is AWARE Hair's highest-leverage DTC channel for reaching affluent 60+ women. 72% of US adults 50+ use Facebook, with 55-64 year-olds spending 45 minutes/day on the platform - the highest engagement of any age group. This strategy outlines a three-tier approach: organic community building, paid acquisition, and AI-powered force multipliers - designed for a bootstrapped team.

Organic Foundation

Budget: $0-500/mo - This is where to start immediately.

2A. Facebook Page Strategy

Content Calendar (3x/week minimum)

DayContent TypeFormatExample
MondayEducationalVideo (1-3 min)"What your scalp is trying to tell you" - Tina explains
WednesdaySocial ProofImage carousel / videoSalon partner testimonial, before/after with context
FridayCommunity / StoryText post / short videoJade's personal story, customer spotlight, myth-busting

Content Pillars

  • Scalp Education (40%): 4R Protocol explainers, environmental factors, ingredient deep-dives. Frame as "what your stylist wishes you knew."
  • Real Results (25%): Before/after sets with context, salon partner video testimonials, customer stories. No filters, no hype.
  • Founder Authority (20%): Tina's trichology expertise, Jade's reactive scalp journey, behind-the-scenes of product development.
  • Lifestyle/Community (15%): Conversations about hair confidence at every age, seasonal tips, "ask a trichologist" prompts.

2B. Facebook Group Strategy

Highest-ROI organic play. Research shows no dominant Facebook Group exists for 60+ women focused on scalp/hair health. This is white space. Target: 500 members in 90 days, 2,000 by end of 2026.

Group Concept: "Scalp Health After 50"

Positioning: Educational community, NOT a brand page. Tina serves as resident expert. Value proposition: "A community where women 50+ discuss scalp health, share what works, and get expert guidance from professionals."

Weekly Rhythm

  • Monday: "Ask Tina" Q&A thread
  • Wednesday: Educational post (scalp care tip, ingredient spotlight, myth vs fact)
  • Friday: Community share (members post their hair journeys)

Launch Sequence

  1. Create group with Tina as primary admin (professional credibility)
  2. Seed with 20-30 initial members from existing customer base and salon partners
  3. Post welcome content: Tina's introduction video, community guidelines, first educational post
  4. Establish weekly rhythms (see above)
  5. Invite salon partners to share with their 50+ clients
  6. Cross-promote from AWARE's Facebook page and email list

Growth Tactics

  • Invite existing Klaviyo subscribers 50+ to join
  • Salon partners share group link with interested clients
  • Create "shareable" educational graphics optimized for group sharing
  • Run occasional Live sessions with Tina answering real questions
  • Collaborate with complementary brands (clean beauty, wellness) for cross-promotion

Moderation Rules

  • No MLM/pyramid scheme promotion
  • No medical claims or diagnosis
  • No spam or self-promotion without permission
  • All AWARE product mentions must be contextual and helpful, never pushy
  • AWARE team members identified transparently

2C. Participating in Existing Groups

Strategy: Be helpful, not promotional. Identify 10-15 active groups covering hair thinning after menopause, clean beauty, women's wellness over 50, and scalp conditions.

Red Lines

  • Never fake grassroots participation (be transparent about AWARE connection)
  • Never copy-paste the same response across groups
  • Never DM group members unsolicited
  • Always prioritize being genuinely helpful over promoting

Paid Acquisition Strategy

Deferred until funding prerequisites are met. The strategy below is ready to deploy when capital constraints lift. See Financial Reality Check for trigger conditions.

Funnel Architecture

  • Dynamic product ads (DPA)

Key Insight: For this demographic and price point ($45-65), a lead magnet funnel (Scalp Environment Report) will likely outperform direct-to-purchase ads. Educated buyers convert at higher rates and have better LTV.

Core Audience

  • Age: 55-70 (primary), 50-75 (broad test)
  • Gender: Women
  • Location: United States
  • Affluence signals: Luxury brand interests, frequent travelers, homeowners

Interest Stacking

  • Hair care + products, Hair coloring (54.1M users, skews mature)
  • Scalp care, Clean beauty / natural products
  • Wellness and holistic health
  • Professional hair salons (not budget chains)
  • Luxury fashion and beauty brands

Lookalike Audiences (build over time)

  1. Value-based lookalike from top 25% customers by LTV - highest priority (71% lower cost-per-opportunity in case studies)
  2. Website visitors who viewed product pages (1%)
  3. Klaviyo email engagers (1%)
  4. Purchasers (1%)
  5. Scalp Environment Report completers (1%)

Geographic Strategy

  1. Radius around salon partners (best for BOFU): 10-25 mile radius around 35+ salon locations
  2. Affluent metro DMAs (best for TOFU): SF, LA, OC, SD, NYC, Boston, DC, Chicago, Miami
  3. Zip code targeting: Median household income $100K+
  4. Advantage+ broad geo: Let Meta optimize, double down on winners

Placement Strategy

  • Facebook Feed: 60-70% of spend (skews older, highest engagement for 60+)
  • Facebook Reels: 10-15% (test only, skews younger)
  • Instagram Feed: 10-15% (secondary)
  • Avoid Audience Network for initial testing

Formats That Work for 60+

  1. Video testimonials (top performer): 30-60 seconds, real customers/salon partners, shot naturally, captions required
  2. Before/after carousels: Clear photos, timeline stated, products listed. Positive framing only.
  3. Educational video ads: Tina explaining a scalp concept in 15-30 seconds
  4. UGC-style content: 36.8% of top beauty ads are UGC format
  5. Static image + testimonial quote: Simple, clear, large text

Headlines That Work

  • "Your Scalp Environment Affects Your Hair More Than You Think"
  • "A Trichologist With 40 Years of Experience Developed This"
  • "Why Salon Professionals Are Switching to Scalp-First Care"
  • "Get Your Free Personalized Scalp Environment Report"
  • "What Hard Water Is Doing to Your Hair (And What to Do About It)"

Before/After Policy VERIFIED

Meta prohibits before/after for wrinkle treatments, Botox, weight loss. However, non-permanent beauty products like hair care CAN use before/after images as long as they use positive framing. AWARE's products are likely compliant.

AI-Powered Force Multipliers

This is where a 1-person team competes with brands that have 10+ people on social.

Content Generation Pipeline

Step 1: Film 2-3 raw videos of Tina/Jade (30 min session)
   |
Step 2: AI-assisted editing (OpusClip, CapCut) - auto-clip, captions, resize
   |
Step 3: Claude generates 10-15 post variations per video
   |
Step 4: AI generates static image variants for A/B testing
   |
Step 5: Schedule via Meta Business Suite or N8N automation

Output: 2-3 hours filming = 15-20 pieces of content

N8N Automation Workflows

Build on existing Railway-hosted N8N instance:

1. Automated Posting Pipeline

Trigger: Google Sheets row (content calendar)
-> Format post for Facebook API
-> Post to Page via Graph API
-> Log post ID and timestamp
-> Schedule engagement check (24h later)

2. Comment Monitoring + Response Drafting

Trigger: Scheduled check (every 2 hours)
-> Pull new comments via Graph API
-> Route to Claude for draft response
-> Send draft for human approval
-> Post approved response

3. Lead Capture Pipeline

Trigger: New Lead Ad submission
-> Sync to Klaviyo via webhook
-> Tag: facebook_lead_ad
-> Trigger Scalp Report flow
-> Sync to Meta Custom Audience

4. Performance Dashboard

Trigger: Daily at 8am
-> Pull Facebook Insights
-> Pull Klaviyo metrics for FB leads
-> Pull Shopify orders with FB UTM
-> Compile daily summary + alerts

5. Social Listening for Scalp Conversations

Trigger: Scheduled (every 6 hours)
-> Search Graph API for scalp care mentions
-> Filter for relevant conversations
-> Draft helpful response suggestions
-> Send for manual review + posting

AI Guerilla Tactics (Ethical)

  1. Scalp Environment Report as Viral Content: Public "Check Your Zip Code" tool optimized for Facebook sharing. Personalized results = social currency.
  2. AI-Powered UGC Amplification: Claude identifies best Judge.me reviews, auto-formats into Facebook graphics. "Customer Spotlight" 2x/week.
  3. Competitor Gap Monitoring: Social listening for competitor complaints. Draft helpful (non-promotional) responses that build authority.
  4. Educational Content Multiplication: One Tina video becomes: Facebook post, 5 reply templates, blog post, email, group prompt. 60-75% time savings.
  5. Personalized Salon Outreach: AI drafts messages referencing each salon's local environmental data. Individually researched, not mass spam.
  6. Facebook Live Automation: Pre-Live Q&A prep, real-time comment queuing, post-Live recap generation.

Your Scalp is Skin

Think of your scalp like a garden - the hair is what grows above the surface, but everything depends on the soil underneath. Compared to your face, the scalp has more hair follicles, more sebaceous (oil) glands, more sweat, and more friction from brushing and styling. Product buildup is heavier - dry shampoo, oils, styling polymers layer on. And it is a covered environment: warm and humid under hair, so the microbiome shifts more easily.

Because the scalp is hidden, problems develop silently - tightness, buildup, inflammation - until they show up as thinning, breakage, or irritation. Healthy hair starts with healthy skin underneath. Today we'll learn to read the signals your scalp is already sending.

2

The 4 Pillars of Scalp Health

◎

Barrier

The Scalp's "Roof"

Skin cells + lipids that keep the good in and the bad out. When compromised, the scalp becomes porous and reactive.

When off: Tightness after washing, stinging with products, redness.

◈

Microbiome

The Ecosystem

Bacteria + yeast that live on your scalp naturally. Not sterile, balanced. Problems come from shifts, not dirt.

When off: Recurrent flakes, waxy scaling, itch that returns fast after washing.

⚠

Inflammation

The Alarm System

An immune response - the scalp saying something is irritating or stressing it. The bridge between discomfort and hair issues.

When off: Itch you can't ignore, warmth/"sunburn" feeling, tenderness at crown.

⟳

Circulation

Traffic + Tension

Follicles need blood flow for nutrients and oxygen. When circulation is restricted, growth slows or stops.

When off: Tight scalp, tension headaches, thinning at crown where galea is thickest.

Your Scalp Care System

The complete at-home routine, designed for consistency over intensity

5

The 4R Protocol

R1

Reset

9·1 Shampoo

Purify the scalp. Focus on scalp, not lengths. Double cleanse: first wash lifts, second treats.

R2

Repair

9·1 Conditioner

Restore moisture + nourish the microbiome. Doubles as a scalp mask.

R3

Reinforce

9·1 Leave-In

Protect from UV, pollution, and heat. Seal in moisture all day.

R4

Reactivate

Gua Sha

Stimulate circulation and release tension. Promotes scalp wellness and healthier hair growth.

Key Principle: Move the scalp tissue, not just the hair. Flat surface makes contact. If it feels like scraping, ease up. This should feel like a structured scalp massage.
Do not gua sha when: Open sores, sunburn, active infection, severe flare-ups, or immediately after chemical services. If it causes pain, stop.

Common Mistakes

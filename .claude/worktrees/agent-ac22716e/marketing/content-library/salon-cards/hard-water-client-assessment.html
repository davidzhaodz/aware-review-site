Hard Water Client Assessment

Identifying and addressing mineral buildup in your clients' hair

85% of US households have hard water [USGS]

Water Hardness Levels

LevelConcentrationWhat You Notice
Soft0-60 mg/LSoap lathers easily, no residue
Moderate61-120 mg/LSome spotting, slight film on skin
Hard121-180 mg/LScale buildup, soap scum, dry hair
Very Hard180+ mg/LHeavy deposits, stiff hair, dullness

Assessment Checklist

  • Hair feels dull and flat despite regular washing
  • Tangles easily, feels coarse or straw-like
  • Color fades fast, pulls warm, or takes unevenly
  • Scalp feels tight or dry even after conditioning
  • Increased shedding, especially during/after washing
  • Products seem to stop working
  • Hair feels noticeably different when traveling

3 Damage Mechanisms

  • Mineral coating - calcium and magnesium form invisible layer, blocking moisture and product absorption
  • pH disruption - hard water pH (7.5-8.5) pushes scalp away from optimal range (4.5-5.5), weakening barrier
  • Follicle clogging - minerals combine with sebum and residue around follicle openings

Client Questions

"Can hard water cause hair loss?"
Not directly, but mineral buildup can clog follicles, disrupt the barrier, and make strands more brittle. Addressing it can reduce breakage and shedding.
Recommend: 9-1 Daily Scalp Cleanser (chelating support, pH 5.5) + shower filter (KDF or activated carbon)
When: Client has 3+ checklist items above, lives in hard water area, or color fades fast

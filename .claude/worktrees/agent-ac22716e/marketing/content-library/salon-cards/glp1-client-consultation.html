Supporting clients experiencing hair changes on weight loss medications

28MAmericans on GLP-1 medications
~1 in 3may experience hair shedding
3-6 motypical recovery timeline

What Is Happening

  • Shedding is most commonly telogen effluvium, triggered by rapid weight loss and nutritional changes, not a direct drug effect
  • Clinical trials reported hair changes in ~3% of semaglutide participants vs 1% placebo
  • Higher shedding rates correlate with greater amounts of weight lost
  • The condition is usually temporary, resolving as weight and nutrition stabilize

Your Role: Recognize, Reassure, Support

Do

  • Acknowledge the concern warmly
  • Explain that shedding is common and usually temporary
  • Focus on scalp environment support
  • Recommend gentle cleansing and barrier protection
  • Suggest protein intake discussion with their doctor

Do Not

  • Suggest adjusting medication
  • Diagnose the cause of hair loss
  • Promise regrowth timelines
  • Recommend supplements without medical guidance
  • Minimize the emotional impact
Refer to Dermatologist If: Patchy (not diffuse) thinning, shedding continuing beyond 6 months, shedding that began before starting medication, or visible scalp in previously full areas.
Recommend: 9-1 Daily Scalp Cleanser (gentle, sulfate-free) + 9-1 Hydrating Scalp Mask + Sandalwood Gua Sha Comb (circulation)
When: Client mentions GLP-1 medication and hair changes

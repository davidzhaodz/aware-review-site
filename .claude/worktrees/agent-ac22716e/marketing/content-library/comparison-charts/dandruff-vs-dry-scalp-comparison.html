Dandruff vs. Dry Scalp

The flakes look the same from a distance, but up close they tell completely different stories. Here is how to tell which one you are dealing with.

Factor Dandruff Dry Scalp
Cause Malassezia yeast overgrowth combined with excess oil production Insufficient moisture and a damaged scalp barrier
Flake type Larger, greasy or sticky, may clump together Smaller, dry, powdery, fall off easily
Flake color Yellowish to white, often with an oily sheen White, dry, and dust-like
Oil level Increased oil production, scalp feels oily or greasy Normal or reduced oil, scalp feels tight or rough
Itch pattern Moderate to severe, often persistent throughout the day Mild to moderate, typically worse after washing
Scalp appearance May have patches of redness or visible irritation in oily zones Minimal redness, more general sensitivity and tightness
Worsened by Stress, irregular washing, oily skin, cold weather (compensatory oil production) Low humidity, indoor heating, hot water, harsh shampoos, hard water
Treatment approach Antifungal ingredients (zinc pyrithione, ketoconazole) to control yeast; gentle oil management Hydration and barrier repair with sulfate-free, pH-balanced cleansers; environmental adjustments

Rich with blood vessels, nerve endings, and collagen. Where the follicle lives.

The Symptoms You Hear

1. "I'm Shedding" / "My Hair Is Thinning"
Could be stress, hormonal, traction from tight styling, or scalp environment. Ask questions first.
2. "I Have Dandruff" / "My Scalp Is Flaking"
Not all flaking is the same. Dry white flakes vs. oily yellow flakes need different approaches.
3. "My Scalp Itches"
Usually inflammation or a reaction. Could be build-up, product sensitivity, stress. Don't dismiss it.
4. "I Have Build-Up"
Product residue, sebum, hard water. You can see it at the bowl. Directly in your wheelhouse.
5. "My Scalp Is Red / Burns After Color"
Could be a product reaction or compromised barrier. Know what was used and when it started.

5 Questions to Ask Your Client

Before you try to figure out what's going on, gather information first.

  • What products are you using at home? - The most revealing question. What's clogging pores or stripping the scalp?
  • Have you been under a lot of stress? - Stress directly affects the scalp through cortisol and tension.
  • How's your diet been? - Iron, vitamin D, protein deficiencies show up as thinning.
  • Are you exercising regularly? - Exercise improves circulation everywhere, including the scalp.
  • Has anything changed recently? - New medications, products, routine? Change is often the trigger.

These questions aren't about selling. They're about understanding your client as a whole person.

The 4-Step Technique

Move the scalp tissue, not just the hair. Light to medium pressure. 8-10 strokes per area.

1
Hairline (GV24 - Spirit Court)
Front to back toward crown. Releases frontalis muscle. Opens face-to-scalp circulation.
2
Crown (GV20 - Baihui)
Circular or radial outward strokes. Galea is thickest here. Remember this name - saying "Baihui" builds instant credibility.
3
Temples (GB8 - Leading Valley)
Upward toward crown + small circles. Where jaw tension meets the scalp.
4
Nape (GB20 - Wind Pool)
Upward toward crown + outward along skull base. Connects to neck and shoulder tension.

AWARE HAIR 18

How to Have the Conversation

"I've noticed some changes at your hairline over the last few visits. I'd feel better if you had a dermatologist take a look. Think of it as a team effort - I'll keep supporting your scalp health from the salon side."

"What you're describing sounds like it might benefit from a dermatologist's input. You want someone who can rule out anything medical while we keep up the scalp care routine."

Never diagnose. Never alarm. Describe what you see, not what it is clinically. Position the referral as additive.

When to Recommend Medical Follow-Up

Refer Promptly
  • Sudden patchy hair loss
  • Pain, burning, or tenderness at the scalp
  • Scarring or smooth, shiny patches with no follicles
  • Pustules, crusting, or non-healing sores
  • Lesions that look different from surrounding scalp
Monitor 4+ Weeks
  • Chronic flaking unresponsive to gentle cleansing
  • Progressive thinning accelerating visit to visit
  • Persistent redness not correlated with products
  • Itching that disrupts daily life

Editor Mailers

What Good Mailers Look Like
  • Ask before sending. Post-pandemic, editors actively resent unsolicited packages. Jen emails first: "Can I send you our new scalp care line launching at C.O. Bigelow?" A "yes" means they'll actually open it.
  • Send 1-3 products, not the full line. The leave-in (most unique SKU) + shampoo (entry point) is a good hero pair. Save the full ritual for editors who express genuine interest.
  • Full-size, not minis. Minis signal you don't trust the product to earn coverage on its own merits.
  • Include one page only. Concise brand story, key ingredient (AWAREBIO Complex), the Bigelow hook, and Awin affiliate info. Not a press kit binder.
  • Sustainable packaging. No confetti, no branded merch, no elaborate unboxing theater. Clean box, clean note, clean product.
  • Volume: 20-40 targeted editors, not 100+ mass mailings. Relevance beats reach at our stage.

Desk-Sides

What a Desk-Side Actually Is
  • A 15-30 minute in-person meeting at an editor's office (or in our case, at Bigelow itself).
  • Bring Tina. A founder with trichologist credentials doing a live scalp assessment is an experience an editor can't get from a mailer. This is the differentiator.
  • Bring 1-2 hero products and a one-page media sheet. Not the full line.
  • Keep it short. Under 20 minutes. Editors' biggest fear is a desk-side that turns into an hour-long presentation. Respect their time.
  • Leave behind: Product samples + one-pager. Send a handwritten thank-you note after.
  • At Bigelow: "We'd love to show you what's new at Bigelow" is a stronger invitation than a generic brand pitch. Editors know Bigelow - the setting does half the selling.

Why the Bigelow Story Matters More Than Product Features

Editorial Reality

For a brand at our revenue stage ($125K), editors won't write about product features alone - there are thousands of new beauty brands. What they WILL write about is:

  • The retail placement. "New brand at America's oldest apothecary" is a headline. "New scalp care shampoo" is not.
  • The founder credential. A master trichologist with 40+ years behind the chair creating a brand is a genuine story. Most beauty founders are marketers, not practitioners.
  • The category angle. Scalp care as the "skinification of hair" is an active editorial beat. Editors are looking for brands to illustrate this trend.

The Bigelow partnership is the thing that makes AWARE press-worthy right now. Without it, we're one of thousands of indie brands competing for the same roundup spots. With it, we have a story.

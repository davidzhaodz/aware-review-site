The Entrepreneurial Mindset:
A Framework for Life

David Zhao, Co-Founder of AWARE Hair  /  Zoom, ~30 min + Q&A  /  Ties into The Innovator's DNA

Hey Kevin, here's a high-level outline of what I'm planning to cover. The central idea is that the five discovery skills from The Innovator's DNA aren't just tools for starting companies - they're a way of approaching any role, any project, any problem. I'll walk through my own career as a case study, showing where each skill showed up, even when I had no intention of "being an entrepreneur."

I have more stories than I'll need, so I'll pick 3-4 to go deeper on and reference the rest. Happy to adjust emphasis based on where the class is in the book or what you think would land best. Let me know what you think.

Core Thesis

Entrepreneurship isn't a career path - it's a lens. It's the habit of looking at anything in front of you and asking: can this be better? That instinct doesn't require a company, a product, or funding. It just requires the decision to care enough to try. And every time you act on it - whether you're automating a spreadsheet or launching a brand - you're creating value that didn't exist before.

Opening ~3 min

Open with a question: "How many of you think you need to start a company to be an entrepreneur?" Quick intro, then frame the talk - my career mapped to the five discovery skills, showing that entrepreneurial thinking can happen inside a consulting firm, a government ministry, or a telecom startup just as much as inside your own company.

Career Stories ~18 min

Each story connects to one or two of the five discovery skills. I'll reference the framework throughout but won't re-teach it - more of a "here's what this looks like in practice."

  • University of Waterloo
    Networking + Associating
    Growing up in Canada's startup ecosystem. Student VC fund, pitch competitions, being surrounded by builders - and how that environment rewires the way you think, even before you build anything yourself.
  • Deloitte Internship
    Observing + Questioning
    Watching a team spend 3 hours a day on a manual Excel process, asking "why?", and automating it with macros. The difference between doing the task you're given and improving the system around it.
  • Khazanah Americas, San Francisco
    Experimenting + Associating
    Sovereign wealth fund internship evaluating $800M-$2B deals. On the side, built a Python model using Bloomberg data that predicted private company valuations from public market signals - nobody asked me to. The entrepreneurial instinct to just go build something when you see a question worth answering.
  • Oliver Wyman, New York
    Questioning + Experimenting
    Consulting for a major US insurer. Built data pipelines that fundamentally changed how the company processed and flagged policy data - proving that even inside massive organizations, "what if we tried this differently?" can move the needle.
  • Oliver Wyman, Dubai + Bahrain Government
    Associating + Experimenting
    COVID hit, moved to the Middle East, ended up advising Bahrain's Ministry of Finance. Government decisions were running on a 30-45 day data lag. Built a real-time GDP dashboard that consolidated data across ministries - taking private-sector analytics practices into a completely different world.
  • Oliver Wyman / Oodi (Zain Iraq) - Reinventing the Sales Funnel
    Observing + Experimenting
    New digital telecom brand in Iraq, where every carrier sells prepaid SIMs through the same convenience stores - zero differentiation, zero loyalty. Instead of fighting for shelf space, we recruited our best customers as brand ambassadors, equipped them with activation devices, and deployed them at train stations and markets. Turned the acquisition model from passive retail into relationship-driven growth. The question: "what if our customers ARE the distribution channel?"
  • AWARE Hair - Everything Converges
    All five skills
    Co-founded a professional scalp care brand where all five skills compound - associating (consulting + tech + beauty), questioning ("why is scalp care an afterthought?"), observing (how salons actually work), networking (co-founder with 25+ years in the industry), and experimenting (constantly testing new tools, channels, and approaches).
AI and the Entrepreneurial Toolkit ~5 min

A practical look at how I use AI tools (specifically Claude Code) as a non-technical founder to run a brand that would otherwise need a much larger team. Quick examples:

  • AI assistant that synthesizes my calendar, tasks, and emails into a morning brief
  • Automated B2B sales pipeline - discovers and qualifies salon leads across the country
  • Custom lead generation tool that pulls real-time environmental data by zip code
  • Financial dashboards, workflow automation, content creation

The message: I'm not a developer - I'm a consultant who learned to ask the right questions. This is the best time in history to be entrepreneurial because AI has collapsed the gap between "I have an idea" and "I built it." Encourage students to start a side project now - a website, an app, anything.

Closing ~2 min

Bring it back to the thesis. The five skills from The Innovator's DNA aren't something you learn once - they're a practice. And the act of being in this class, studying these ideas, already makes you more entrepreneurial than most. No matter where you end up after graduation, the people who keep asking "can this be better?" are the ones who end up shaping things.

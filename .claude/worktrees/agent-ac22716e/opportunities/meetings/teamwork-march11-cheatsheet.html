Red Lines vs. Flexibility

Non-Negotiable

  • Boom Labs agreement remains primary and controlling
  • Teamwork agreement scoped to NDA/confidentiality ONLY
  • No actual formulas or specs in the Teamwork agreement
  • No purchase order or manufacturing services provisions
  • AWARE retains right to choose its manufacturing partner
  • No "Entire Agreement" clause that could override Boom Labs
  • We do not consent to assignment of the Boom Labs contract

Open to Discussion

  • Timeline for future Teamwork manufacturing contract
  • Specific NDA terms (duration, scope of coverage)
  • Indemnification language protecting Teamwork from liability
  • Non-compete scope (how broad the derivative formula ban is)
  • Insurance requirements Teamwork already carries
  • Preliminary discussion of future manufacturing pricing
  • Competing projects notification scope
Their Arguments & Our Responses
They'll Say Our Response
"Seyed works for us now, so the manufacturing relationship should be with us." Seyed's employment doesn't change our contract. Our agreement is with Boom Lab Inc. as an entity. We respect his choice to join Teamwork, but that's exactly why we need a separate NDA - to protect everyone.
"We can offer better terms, more capacity, streamlined operations." Great - let's explore that for future runs. Right now the priority is the confidentiality framework. Once Boom Labs commitments are complete, we sit down on manufacturing terms.
"The V2 just formalizes what's already happening on the ground." If manufacturing happens at your facility, that's why we need the NDA. But the manufacturing agreement itself stays with the entity we contracted with. Two separate relationships.
"Boom Labs is just Seyed - we're the real operation." We understand the practical dynamics. Legally, our contract is with Boom Lab Inc. We need to maintain that structure. The NDA protects the information flowing through your facility and team.
"We need a manufacturing agreement for our own liability and compliance." A confidentiality agreement can include appropriate liability protections. We can work on indemnification language. But it should be scoped to confidentiality, not manufacturing services.
"We're taking over Boom Labs' operations." Any assignment requires our written consent per the contract. We haven't consented. Let's focus on the NDA, which benefits everyone right now.
Concession Strategy
When to Play
After Pushback
Only after firmly establishing NDA-only scope AND they push back wanting a manufacturing relationship.
The Offer
Future Mfg Contract
After current Boom Labs commitments are fulfilled, we negotiate a direct manufacturing agreement with Teamwork.
Conditions
NDA Signed First
NDA must be signed and in good standing. Current Boom Labs production must be completed. Terms negotiated in good faith.
The Concession Script
"We understand where you're coming from. You've built the infrastructure, you employ Seyed, and you want a direct relationship. That makes sense. Here's what we propose: let's get this confidentiality agreement signed now to protect everyone. Then, once our current production commitments with Boom Labs are fulfilled, we sit down and negotiate a manufacturing agreement directly with Teamwork. We're committed to that conversation."
Do NOT Promise
Specific pricing or volume for the future contract. A specific transition date. Exclusivity with Teamwork. That future terms will mirror Boom Labs terms. Anything that implies Boom Labs contract is voided.

Key Lines
Core Position
"Our manufacturing contract is with Boom Labs. That's a signed, active agreement. What we need with Teamwork is a complementary confidentiality agreement that protects our IP when your employees encounter our proprietary information. It's not a replacement - it's an addition."
On the V2 Scope
"We'd like to work together on a focused confidentiality agreement. We don't need manufacturing services terms, purchase order provisions, or quality control sections in this document - those are covered by our existing manufacturing arrangement."
On Formulas in the Document
"We can't include our actual formula specifications in a confidentiality agreement. The formulas were disclosed under our Boom Labs contract. This agreement should define what's confidential without documenting the secrets themselves."

"We understand you have leverage as the facility. But Boom Labs has contractual obligations to deliver, and we have remedies under that agreement. We'd prefer to work together collaboratively. That's why we're here."

"Any transfer or assignment of our Boom Labs agreement requires our written consent. We haven't given that consent. If there's a change in Boom Labs' corporate structure, that's a conversation we need to have directly with Boom Labs."

Once that's signed and our current Boom Labs commitments are complete, we're committed to sitting down and discussing a direct manufacturing relationship with Teamwork.

Background
The Situation
Seyed Now Works for Teamwork
Our chemist Seyed was independent when we signed our manufacturing contract with his company, Boom Labs. He has since become a Teamwork/Labayk employee. Teamwork now wants to take over the entire manufacturing relationship.
Why This Meeting
Protect Our Formulas
Teamwork employees handle our batch cards and see our formulas during production. We need them to sign a confidentiality agreement. But we do NOT want to give them control of the manufacturing relationship.
How They See Our Formulas
AWARE owns the formulas. We disclosed them to Boom Labs (Seyed) under our manufacturing contract. Seyed creates batch cards for production. Those batch cards are physically handled by Teamwork employees at the facility - they weigh ingredients, follow mixing instructions, and see our formulas in practice. They already have exposure. The confidentiality agreement binds them directly so we have legal recourse if anything goes wrong.

We Have a Draft NDA Ready
Our Proposed Agreement Covers
Confidentiality obligations for Teamwork employees who handle our information. Non-disclosure, non-use, and formula derivation prohibitions. Return/destruction of materials. Direct legal recourse if breached. It does NOT include manufacturing terms, purchase orders, quality control, or our actual formula specifications.
Red Lines vs. Flexibility

We Will Not

  • Transfer or reassign the Boom Labs contract to Teamwork
  • Sign anything that makes Teamwork our "manufacturer"
  • Include our actual formulas in any Teamwork agreement
  • Give Teamwork authority over purchase orders or production decisions
  • Promise exclusivity for future manufacturing
  • Set a specific date for transitioning away from Boom Labs

We're Open To

  • Discussing a future manufacturing contract with Teamwork after current Boom Labs commitments are fulfilled
  • Adjusting the NDA terms (duration, scope, indemnification language)
  • Preliminary conversations about future manufacturing pricing
  • Acknowledging Teamwork's role in the production process
  • Competing projects notification to give them comfort

"We need a manufacturing agreement for our own liability protection." We can include liability protections in the confidentiality agreement. Indemnification language, scope of responsibility - we're flexible on that. It just needs to be scoped to the confidentiality relationship, not manufacturing services.
"We're taking over Boom Labs' operations / Boom Labs is being folded in." Any transfer of our contract requires our written consent. We haven't been approached about that. If Boom Labs' corporate structure is changing, that's a conversation we'd need to have with them directly.
Our Concession Card
When to Use
Only After Pushback
Don't lead with this. Only offer it if they push back hard on wanting a manufacturing relationship and the conversation stalls.
The Offer
Future Manufacturing Contract
After our current Boom Labs production commitments are complete, we'll negotiate a direct manufacturing agreement with Teamwork for future runs.
How to Say It
"We understand where you're coming from. You've built the infrastructure, you employ Seyed, and you want a direct relationship. That makes sense. Here's what we propose: let's get this confidentiality agreement signed now to protect everyone. Then, once our current production commitments with Boom Labs are fulfilled, we sit down and negotiate a manufacturing agreement directly with Teamwork. We're committed to that conversation."
Boundaries on the Concession
Do not commit to specific pricing or volume. Do not give a specific transition date. Do not promise exclusivity. Do not say the future contract will mirror current Boom Labs terms. The message is: "We're open to it, and it's a real conversation - after the NDA is signed and current commitments are met."

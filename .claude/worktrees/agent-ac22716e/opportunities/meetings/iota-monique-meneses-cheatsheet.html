About Monique Meneses
Monique Meneses
Co-Founder & Co-CEO, Iota Body Care

Background

  • Brown University graduate
  • Executive Editor, Allure & allure.com (10+ years beauty editorial)
  • Beauty editor career gave her deep media/editor network
  • BeautyMatter NEXT50 2025 honoree

Personal Story

Half Korean, quarter Filipina, quarter Portuguese. After facing infertility struggles and a pancreas tumor diagnosis, she realized how little she knew about what she was putting on her body. Couldn't find bodycare that met the same ingredient standards as her skincare. Co-founded Iota with her husband Ray Kim. Their daughter Genevieve has been involved from the start.

Superpower

10+ years as beauty editor = built-in PR network. Knows every editor, journalist, and influencer in beauty media. This is likely why Iota has racked up 24 awards - she knew exactly how to position products for editorial recognition.

Co-Founder: Ray Kim

Her husband and co-CEO. Background as an advisor and investor in consumer, media, and technology sectors. Together they run Iota as a husband-wife team - lean, bootstrapped, no outside investors.

Rapport note: Monique's origin story (health scare leading to product creation) parallels Tina's trichology-driven approach. Both brands were born from personal experience, not market analysis. She'll understand the founder journey.

Questions to Ask
1 Early Days & Bootstrapping
  • Must
    "You launched in Oct 2022 with no outside funding. What were the hardest decisions in those first 6-12 months about where to spend vs. where to save?"
    AWARE is at this exact stage. Understanding capital allocation early on.
  • Must
    "How did you and Ray divide responsibilities early on? What did you handle yourselves vs. outsource?"
    David + Tina have a similar co-founder dynamic.
  • Should
    "At what revenue point did bootstrapping start feeling sustainable vs. scary? Was there a moment it clicked?"
2 PR, Awards & Editorial
  • Must
    "You've won 24 awards - which is extraordinary for a brand this young. How much of that was your editorial network vs. a deliberate awards submission strategy?"
    Trying to understand if there's a replicable playbook beyond her Allure connections.
  • Must
    "For a brand without editorial connections, what would you recommend as the best path to getting press and award consideration?"
  • Should
    "Did the awards drive meaningful revenue, or was it more about credibility and retail conversations?"
  • Bonus
    "Did you hire a PR agency or handle it in-house? If agency, who and at what stage?"
3 Retail Strategy
  • Must
    "How did the Credo partnership come about? Did they find you, or did you pitch them? What made them say yes?"
    Understanding how a bootstrapped brand lands its first major retail partner.
  • Must
    "How do you decide which retailers to pursue vs. pass on? You're in Credo and Nordstrom but not Sephora or Ulta - is that deliberate?"
  • Should
    "What surprised you most about the retail side of the business? Things you didn't anticipate?"
  • Should
    "AWARE is B2B-first (salons). Any thoughts on whether that's a harder or easier path to retail than going DTC-first like you did?"
4 Team & Operations
  • Should
    "You've stayed lean - husband-wife core team. What was the first role you hired or contracted for, and when did you know you needed it?"
  • Should
    "The scientific advisory board with Brown and Harvard researchers is impressive. How did you recruit them, and what do they actually do day-to-day?"
    AWARE has Tina's trichology expertise but could benefit from a formal advisory board.
  • Bonus
    "How do you manage manufacturing and supply chain as a small team? Any hard lessons?"
5 Marketing & Social
  • Must
    "I've seen Iota get a lot of social traction - has there been a specific viral moment or piece of content that really moved the needle? Was it planned or organic?"
    Understanding if viral social is a strategy or just lightning in a bottle.
  • Should
    "How do you think about paid vs. organic marketing? At what stage did you start spending on ads, if at all?"
  • Should
    "What content approach has worked best for building community - educational, lifestyle, UGC, influencer?"
  • Bonus
    "You created a new category - 'nutritional bodycare.' How did you get people to understand a category that didn't exist?"
    AWARE faces the same challenge with 'scalp wellness.'
6 Revenue & Growth
  • Must
    "What were the biggest revenue milestones - the moments where growth really accelerated? Was it a specific retail deal, viral moment, or something else?"
  • Should
    "How does your revenue split between DTC and retail? Has that ratio changed as you've grown?"
  • Bonus
    "Any thoughts on when (or whether) to take outside investment? Have you been approached by VCs?"
7 Mistakes & Lessons
  • Must
    "If you could go back to where AWARE is now - about 6-8 months in, bootstrapped, building traction - what would you do differently?"
    The most valuable question. Specific enough to get a real answer.
  • Should
    "What's been the biggest surprise - positive or negative - about building a beauty brand?"
  • Bonus
    "Is there anything you spent money on early that turned out to be a waste? Or something you wish you'd invested in sooner?"

Follow-Up Ideas

Potential Collaboration

"Scalp + body = complete wellness routine. If the conversation goes well, there might be a natural co-marketing opportunity - Iota for body, AWARE for scalp. Complementary, not competitive. A joint 'skin wellness' positioning."

Introductions

"She likely knows Credo buyers, Nordstrom contacts, beauty editors, and PR firms. Don't ask in the first meeting - but if a real relationship develops, these connections could be invaluable. Earn them by being genuinely helpful first."

Ongoing Relationship

"Offer to be a sounding board for her too - founders at similar stages but different categories can help each other think through challenges. Keep the relationship reciprocal. Follow up with a specific insight or resource she'd find valuable."

After the chat: Send a thank-you note within 24 hours. Reference something specific she said - not generic. If she mentioned a challenge, send a resource that helps. If she recommended something, follow up on it and let her know. Build the relationship before asking for anything.
Remember
Listen 70%, talk 30%. You're here to learn, not to impress. The best coffee chats feel like a conversation between two founders who genuinely understand each other's journey. Be curious, be specific, and be grateful for her time.

Background

Where We Stand

Boom Lab (Seyed Hossein Zoie) is our contract manufacturer, producing shampoo, conditioner, and leave-in through Teamwork Packaging. The original manufacturing agreement was signed July 22, 2024 for 30,000 total units (10,000 per SKU across 4 production runs of 2,500 each).

Seyed requested a price increase citing higher raw material costs (13-18% across SKUs). After review, we agreed to accept his proposed rates. He sent a Pro-Invoice (PI) on March 12, 2026 for Run 2, but it contains several errors that need correction before we approve payment.

Effective Deposit Percentage

At Boom Lab's new (accepted) prices, the total contract value rises to $107,500. Our $43,890 already paid represents 40.8% of that new total, not the 30% shown on the PI. This 40.8% is the effective deposit rate that should be applied going forward to each invoice.

Issues with the PI

What Seyed Sent vs. What It Should Be

The Pro-Invoice dated 3/12/2026 (PI #31220261SHZ) has the following issues:

  • Quantity Wrong PI lists 3,750 units per SKU. Contract specifies 2,500 per SKU per run. That's a 50% overbill.
  • 400 Shampoo Owed Run 1 was 400 shampoo units short. We paid for 2,500 but received 2,100. We should only pay for 2,100 new shampoo units this run (he delivers 2,500, but 400 are already paid for).
  • Fragrance Cost Per our contract, fragrance (scent) costs are on the seller. The PI makes no mention of a fragrance deduction. Our scent (Pacific Air NF from OnScent) costs $73.46/lb - this run requires ~35.7 lbs ($2,623), accounting for the 400 extra shampoo units.
  • Deposit Rate PI applies 30% deposit. Our effective deposit is 40.8% based on actual payments made vs. the repriced contract total.
  • Payment Terms PI says "50% advance, balance before delivery." The signed addendum specifies equal installments split before production and after delivery. This changes our cash flow timing.
  • No Leave-In Only shampoo and conditioner on this PI. Leave-in is part of the contract but not included here. Need to confirm if this is a separate run or intentional hold.

Decision Point

Two Options for Fragrance

The contract states fragrance is on the seller (Boom Lab). However, we have the option to absorb this cost in exchange for a simpler relationship and fewer points of negotiation. Here's what each scenario looks like:

Option A: Deduct Fragrance

Per contract terms. Boom Lab absorbs scent cost.

Difference Between Options

Option A saves $1,552.84 on this run ($8,043 vs. $9,596). Across all remaining runs (2-4), enforcing the fragrance clause saves approximately $5,200-$5,800 total, depending on leave-in volume. However, absorbing it (Option B) removes a friction point and may make the overall relationship smoother, especially since we're already asking for corrections on quantity, the shampoo credit, and the deposit rate.

Questions to Ask Jen

Advice from a fellow founder: Ask how often they pitch and follow up. Get visibility on pitches before they go out - it should be a collaboration, not a black box. If results are slow, you need to understand what's happening behind the scenes.

Priority

  1. Pitch Visibility & Approval "Can we see and approve pitches before they go out? We want to collaborate on the angles, not just get results after the fact."
  2. Pitching Cadence "The SOW says 3-4 pitches per month. Are those 3-4 distinct angles, or variations on one? And how aggressive is the follow-up - once, twice, ongoing?"
  3. Full Reporting (Not Just Wins) "Beyond placement links, will we see what was pitched, to whom, what the response was, and what didn't land? We want visibility into the full process."
  4. The Rootist Overlap "You've worked with The Rootist, which is also in scalp/haircare. How would you differentiate AWARE when pitching to the same editors?"
  5. Salon-Exclusive Angle "We're salon-exclusive - no Sephora, no Amazon. Have you pitched that positioning before? How do editors respond when a product isn't at a major retailer?"
  6. Professional Trade Press "Do you have relationships with trade publications like Modern Salon, Behind the Chair, or American Salon? Those matter a lot to us as a B2B brand."
  7. What You Need From Us "What press materials do you need before we start? Bios, fact sheets, hi-res photos - what's the full list?"
  8. Affiliate Setup "How quickly do we need Awin set up? Is it a blocker for pitching, or can we start while we get it going?"
  9. Awards Deadlines "Any specific awards deadlines in the next 3 months? We've already submitted to Marie Claire and CEW on our own."

If There's Time

  1. First 90 Days "Walk us through what the first 3 months look like month by month."
  2. NYC Without Us "When we're not in NYC, how do you represent the brand? Do you do desksides on our behalf?"
  3. Editorial Calendars "If a magazine is planning a 'best scalp products' story for fall, how far in advance do we need to be pitching?"
  4. Thought Leadership "What thought leadership opportunities do you see for Tina - podcasts, conferences, business press?"

Conversation Guidance

Jen heard David's version of the story. She specifically said the founders telling it themselves is much more powerful. Here's what to lean into:

Tell your story personally. Jade - the hair loss experience and what it meant to find something that worked. Tina - why you created these products after 40+ years, colleagues who got sick from chemicals, products that damage the scalp. The mother-daughter dynamic.
Show your expertise. Jen wants to pitch Tina as a go-to expert for editors. Share what scalp/hair topics you feel strongest on, trends you're seeing in the salon, and any contrarian takes on common hair advice.
Discuss the salon for editor events. Would a Monday editor day work? Back-to-back blowout appointments, light bites, gift bags. What makes the AWARE treatment different from any other salon?
Photography. Editors need good imagery. What do you have? Open to a simple lifestyle shoot? Headshots for press bios?

Things to Keep in Mind

  • Be yourselves. Jen said the founder story is the best sales tool. She wants to hear it from you directly, not a polished pitch.
  • She's scrappy and understands budget constraints. She specifically chose to work with startup brands because she saw them get neglected at big agencies. She's not expecting a big budget.
  • Ask about The Rootist. It's the most relevant case study since they're also in scalp/haircare. Understanding how she positioned them will tell you a lot about how she'd position AWARE.
  • Affiliate is important. Both Jen and at least one other PR person David spoke with said Awin is essential for editor placements. This should probably be a priority to set up regardless of the PR decision.
  • No need to commit today. David has already reviewed the SOW. Today is about you two getting comfortable with Jen and her getting to know the brand from the people who built it. The decision can happen after.

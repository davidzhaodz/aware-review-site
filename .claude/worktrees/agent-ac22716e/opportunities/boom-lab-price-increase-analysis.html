Section 6

Contractual Leverage Points

  • Section 5.4 - Price Adjustments: "Manufacturer agrees that any price adjustments for future orders shall be subject to mutual agreement. Company expects price decreases for increased order volumes and long-term commitments." This clause explicitly protects AWARE from unilateral increases.
  • No Price Escalation Clause: The contract contains no automatic price escalation mechanism tied to inflation, raw materials, or cost pass-throughs. Prices are fixed for the contract term.
  • Unresolved Shortage: 400 shampoo units were paid for in full and not delivered. Any pricing negotiation should be contingent on resolving this first.
  • Payment Imbalance: AWARE has paid 47.5% of contract value but received only 24.7% of units. This overpayment gives significant leverage - AWARE has demonstrated good faith as a buyer.
  • Section 13.4 - Amendments: "This Agreement may be amended or modified only by a written agreement signed by both parties." Any price change requires a formal, signed amendment - Boom Lab cannot simply apply new prices.
  • Section 6.2 - Termination for Cause: If Boom Lab fails to deliver at contracted terms, AWARE has the right to terminate with 30 days notice after breach and failure to cure.
  • Section 11.2 - Unlimited Liability Cap: The Manufacturer's total cumulative liability is uncapped and covers all costs, damages, and losses from breach or negligence. This is unusual and highly favorable to AWARE.
  • Section 12 - Force Majeure: The force majeure clause permits delay or termination only - it does not allow price adjustments. Even if Boom Lab claims external cost pressures, this clause does not support a price increase.
Section 7

Strategic Considerations

Switching Costs

Switching manufacturers would cost an estimated $15,000-$35,000 plus 4-8 months of lead time (new manufacturer search, formula transfer, stability testing, pilot batch). This is roughly in the same range as the proposed price increase itself, making a switch hard to justify purely on economics. The switching argument is stronger if this is about long-term partner reliability.

The Precedent Problem

This matters more than the $11K Accepting 13-18% without negotiation signals that future increases will also be accepted. If AWARE plans to scale to larger orders, establishing that pricing is a two-way negotiation now prevents compounding increases later.

Market Context

In beauty/personal care contract manufacturing (2024-2026), a 5-10% cost increase is within market norms due to raw material, packaging, and labor inflation. The 13-18% requested by Boom Lab is on the high end and should be supported by an itemized cost breakdown. If Boom Lab cannot specify which raw materials, packaging components, or labor costs increased, that may indicate margin-padding rather than genuine cost pass-through.

Pre-Negotiation Checklist

  • Request itemized cost breakdown from Boom Lab - Which specific raw materials, packaging, or labor increased? By how much? Reluctance to provide this is a red flag.
  • Get 2-3 competitive quotes - Even ballpark quotes from alternative manufacturers (PartnerSlate, Maker's Row, direct outreach) provide market rate context and leverage.
  • Confirm formula ownership - Does AWARE fully own the formulations independent of Boom Lab? This determines switching feasibility.
  • Check inventory runway - How many months of stock on hand? This determines urgency and negotiating timeline.
  • Document the 400-unit shampoo shortfall - Gather delivery receipts and any communications acknowledging the shortage.
  • Determine minimum acceptable margins - Know your walk-away price before entering the conversation.

Section 8

Recommended Approach

Lead with Split the Difference (Scenario C)
  1. Open by addressing the shortage - Before any pricing discussion, require confirmation that the 400 shampoo shortage units will be included in the next delivery at no additional cost. Establish that both sides have obligations to meet.
  2. Acknowledge the cost pressure - Show willingness to work with them. Raw material costs have gone up industry-wide, and you understand they need to stay viable as a partner.
  3. Propose Scenario C - Meet them halfway on the increase (~8% vs. their 14-18%). This demonstrates good faith and keeps the relationship collaborative, while saving $5,500+ vs. full acceptance. The additional ~$5.8K cost is manageable and positions you as a fair partner.
  4. If they want more, counter with Scenario E - Pair a 7% increase with a larger volume commitment. This gives them more predictable revenue and a longer-term relationship, saving you an additional ~$900 vs. the split. The contract supports your position since price adjustments require mutual agreement (Section 5.4).

Pre-Negotiation Checklist

  • Gather documentation of the 400-unit shampoo shortage (delivery receipts, communications)
  • Confirm total payments made to Boom Lab to date ($43,890 per this analysis)
  • Research alternative manufacturers for leverage (even if not planning to switch)
  • Determine AWARE's minimum acceptable margins to know your walk-away price
  • Consider whether retail price adjustments are feasible if some increase is accepted

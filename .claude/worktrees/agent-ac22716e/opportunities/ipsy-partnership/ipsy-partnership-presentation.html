The Opportunity

What is IPSY Glam Bag?

One of the largest beauty subscription services in North America. Curated sample-size products delivered to millions of subscribers monthly. Brands pay for manufacturing; IPSY provides the audience. A proven discovery channel for beauty brands at scale.

Brand Exposure

200,000 potential new customers will physically hold and try an AWARE product. This trial-driving exposure would cost $100K-$200K+ in paid digital advertising, with far lower conversion rates than a physical product experience.

Strategic Fit

Premium brands like Oribe and Bumble and bumble have used subscription box sampling without damaging their salon positioning. At 200K units (vs 360K), this reads as curated sampling rather than mass distribution.

Contract Deep Dive - High Risk Clauses
1. Payment Terms (Net 60) Medium

IPSY pays 60 days after delivery window close or warehouse receipt, whichever is later. Payment ~September 15. Labeled "Co-Op Marketing Fees" not product purchase.

Negotiation
Push for Net 30 - moves payment to ~Aug 16, closing the cash gap almost entirely.
2. Late Delivery Penalty High

2% per business day late, capped at 20%. Five days late = $8,600 (erodes but doesn't eliminate expected profit). Ten days late = $17,200.

Negotiation
Ask for 5-day grace period and reduction to 1%/day with 10% cap.
3. QC Penalty High

Flat 10% penalty ($8,600) for ANY non-conforming units. Binary - no proportional scale. "Non-conforming" not defined. Whether 10 or 10,000 units fail, same penalty.

Negotiation
Clarify definition + push for proportional penalty (only applies if >5% of units fail).
6. Indemnification High

Uncapped liability for ALL product claims + cross-brand indemnification (other brands in same Glam Bag). One lawsuit could exceed total deal value.

Assessment
Standard for industry but significant. Confirm insurance coverage with broker. Lower unit count (200K vs 360K) reduces but doesn't eliminate exposure.
Contract Deep Dive - Medium & Low Risk Clauses
4. ExclusivityLow

6 months before + after September: no competing subscription boxes (US/CA/MX). Window: ~March 2026 - March 2027. Does NOT affect salon, DTC, or retail. Acceptable as-is.

5. InsuranceResolved

$1M/$2M CGL + $5M umbrella. A.M. Best A- rating. Already covered - just add IPSY as additional insured ($500-$1,500).

7. IP LicenseMedium

Non-exclusive, worldwide. Survives termination for published content only. Cannot create new content post-termination. Standard.

8. CancellationMedium

90 days' notice for convenience. Anytime for cause. No reimbursement if costs already incurred. Request kill fee.

9. Right of OffsetMedium

IPSY can withhold payments against claimed liabilities. Broad "reasonable determination" language. Document everything meticulously.

10. Recall CostsHigh

100% AWARE - including consumer refunds, replacements, shipping, admin. Still significant at 200K scale. Confirm insurance covers recalls.

11. ReturnsMedium

Undistributed product returned for refund. AWARE pays shipping. Low likelihood - IPSY uses what they order.

12-14. OtherLow

Confidentiality: 3yr post-term, trade secrets perpetual. Law: CA, AAA arbitration SF. Termination: Material breach with cure period.

Negotiation Strategy

With healthier margins, we negotiate from a position of strength. Even one win here adds meaningful cushion.

Net 30 Instead of Net 60Priority 1

Biggest impact. Moves payment from ~Sep 15 to ~Aug 16. Reduces the $25K-$36K cash gap by ~30 days of float. Combined with small credit line, makes the deal much more comfortable.

Ask

"Given significant upfront capital, we'd like Net 30 to manage production cash flow."

Fallback

Milestone payment (30% at PPS approval, 70% Net 30 after delivery). Or Net 45 compromise.

QC Penalty ClarificationPriority 2

Current: binary 10% ($8,600) for ANY non-conforming units. Request proportional penalty (>5% failure threshold) + clear definition of "non-conforming." Exclude minor cosmetic issues.

Late Penalty Grace PeriodPriority 3

Ask: 5-day grace period + 1%/day with 10% cap. Fallback: accept 2%/day with 3-day grace and 10% cap (vs 20%). At 200K units, 5 days late = $8,600 (absorbable) vs 10 days = $17,200 (painful).

Higher Unit Price ($0.48-$0.50)Priority 4

More important now with tighter margins. At $0.48: revenue = $96K (+$10K, expected profit jumps to ~$15.5K). At $0.50: revenue = $100K (+$14K). Would meaningfully improve the economics.

Recommendation

Option A - Proceed As-Is

Viable. Expected profit: $5,507 (6% margin). Cash gap of $25K-$36K requires small credit line. Worst case breaks even. 200K brand impressions as strategic bonus.

Option B - Negotiate Then Proceed

Push for Net 30 (Priority #1 - directly reduces cash gap), QC clarification, penalty grace period. Net 30 alone would move payment ~30 days earlier and improve cashflow significantly.

Recommended

Option C - Decline

Valid if: Out List ingredient conflict, manufacturer can't commit, or strategic positioning concern outweighs opportunity. Less justifiable on financial grounds than in February.

The February deal was a brand exposure play that hoped to break even. The March deal is a profitable partnership with built-in resilience. The Out List is cleared. The two remaining questions: 1) Can Boom Lab commit to a 200K mini run in May-June? 2) Is AWARE ready to operate at this scale?

Walk-Away Conditions: Out List ingredient conflict. Manufacturer can't commit to timeline. IPSY won't budge on any negotiation point AND you're uncomfortable with uncapped indemnification.

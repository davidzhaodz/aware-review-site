Our Leverage

  • Section 5.4: Price adjustments require mutual agreement. Contract explicitly anticipates price decreases with volume. No price escalation clause exists.
  • 400-unit shampoo shortage: Already paid for, not delivered.
  • Payment imbalance: We've paid 47.5% of contract but received 24.7% of units - we are significantly ahead on payments.
  • Section 11.2: Manufacturer liability is uncapped. Section 12 (Force Majeure) permits delay/termination only, not price changes.
  • Section 13.4: Any amendment requires a written agreement signed by both parties.

Recommended Approach

Lead with Split the Difference (Scenario C)
  1. Open with the shortage - Require confirmation that 400 shampoo units will be included in the next delivery at no cost. Establish that both sides have obligations to meet.
  2. Acknowledge the cost pressure - Show willingness to work together. Raw material costs have risen, and you want them to stay viable as a partner.
  3. Propose Scenario C - Meet halfway on the increase (~8% vs. their 14-18%). Demonstrates good faith while saving $5,500+ vs. full acceptance. Additional cost of ~$5.8K is manageable.
  4. Hold firm if they push beyond the split - Contract requires mutual agreement on price changes (Section 5.4). The payment imbalance and 400-unit shortage give us standing to hold at the midpoint.

Get 2-3 competitive quotes from alternative manufacturers for market context. Confirm formula ownership and current inventory runway. Document the 400-unit shampoo shortfall with delivery receipts.

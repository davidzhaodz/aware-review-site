Tiffany De Jesus

BeautySpace - Head of Business Development
Jan 27, 2:00-2:30 PM BV 2630 (Your Booth)

Company Background

  • 900+ distribution points nationwide
  • Partners: Bloomingdale's, Nordstrom, Dillard's, Macy's
  • 125+ beauty brands in portfolio
  • Recently acquired by PCA Companies
  • Focus: Retail distribution, department stores

Strategic Angle

BeautySpace is retail-focused, not salon channel. This meeting is about building relationships, gathering intel on retail buyer expectations, and planting seeds for future expansion. Not an immediate distribution fit.

Conversation Script

[Opening - Tiffany arrives at booth]
Jade
Tiffany, thank you so much for making time to meet. I'm Jade Wong, Co-Founder and COO of AWARE Hair. Welcome to our booth.
Tiffany
Thanks for having me. I saw your brand on the show app and was intrigued by the scalp care positioning. Tell me more about what you're doing.
Jade
AWARE Hair is a professional-exclusive scalp care line founded by Tina Mui - she's a master trichologist with 40 years of experience. She kept seeing the same unsolved problems in her chair: hard water damage, pollution stress, UV damage on the scalp. So she developed a patent-pending formulation using dual-weight hyaluronic acid and Tahitian microalgae to address environmental scalp health.
Tiffany
Interesting. What's your distribution look like currently?
Jade
Right now we're exclusively in salons - 35+ professional partners across LA, Chicago, and NYC. Our focus has been proving product-market fit in the professional channel first. And the results have been incredible: 96% B2B repurchase rate, zero returns across our entire network, and an average reorder cycle of 28-35 days.
Tiffany
96% repurchase? That's exceptionally high. What do you attribute that to?
Jade
Honestly - it works. Stylists see the results on their clients' scalps, clients feel the difference, and they keep asking for it. Our top salon does $12,000 annually with us, and they started with a $1,800 trial order. Within 90 days, they added scalp assessments to every new client consultation.
Tiffany
So you're salon-exclusive currently. What brings you to talk with BeautySpace? We're primarily retail distribution.
Jade
Great question. I know BeautySpace has incredible reach - 900+ distribution points, relationships with Nordstrom, Bloomingdale's, and others. I wanted to understand a few things. First, how do retail buyers evaluate emerging brands in the scalp care space? And second, what would a brand like ours need to demonstrate before retail expansion makes sense?
Tiffany
Smart approach. Most brands come to us too early - they want retail before they've proven anything. The fact that you're leading with salon is actually a strong foundation. Retail buyers want to see: consistent sales velocity, brand awareness or social proof, and a clear value proposition that stands out on shelf.
Jade
That's helpful. We have 18 million organic social views with zero ad spend - the content resonates. But our thesis is that building professional credibility first creates pull demand. When someone gets a great scalp treatment at their salon, they go looking for the product.
Tiffany
That's the Olaplex playbook, and it worked. What's your price point?
Jade
$55-60 retail for our core products - shampoo, conditioner, and leave-in. Premium positioning, clean formulation - sulfate-free, paraben-free, silicone-free, vegan, cruelty-free.
Tiffany
That's a strong price point. Clean and clinical is where the category is moving. Do you have any IP protection?
Jade
Yes - patent-pending on our formulation. The dual-weight hyaluronic acid technology is exclusive to us. We're not just another clean shampoo.
[Transition to Close]
Tiffany
This is a solid foundation. I think you're doing this the right way - building credibility before racing to retail. The salon-exclusive positioning actually makes you more attractive to retail buyers later, not less.
Jade
That means a lot to hear. Can I ask - what would you recommend as our next steps? Not necessarily with BeautySpace specifically, but in building toward retail-readiness?
Tiffany
A few things. First, keep building your salon base - the more professional endorsement, the better. Second, invest in brand awareness - even organic social helps. Third, when you're ready to have a retail conversation, come back with sell-through data from your professional accounts. Retail buyers love seeing velocity.
Jade
That's incredibly helpful. Would you be open to staying in touch? I'd love to update you as we grow, and when the time is right, explore what a retail partnership could look like.
Tiffany
Absolutely. Send me your deck and let's reconnect in Q3 or Q4. By then you'll have more data and we'll have a clearer sense of our 2027 planning.
Jade
Perfect. Here's my card and our one-pager. Can I send you some samples as well? Experiencing the product is really the best way to understand the difference.

Tell me about your products and how they work in a spa treatment setting.

We have three core products that work together as a scalp treatment system. The 9.1 Professional Shampoo preps the scalp with our patent-pending hyaluronic acid complex - it's been clinically shown to deliver 189x more hydration than placebo. The Conditioner strengthens with SymHair Force, reducing breakage by 82%. And the Leave-In provides ongoing environmental protection. For spa settings, we have backbar sizes - 1-liter formats - that are perfect for treatment rooms. Plus travel sizes that guests can purchase to continue their routine.

What does a typical scalp treatment protocol look like?
Jade
A full head spa treatment using AWARE products takes about 30-45 minutes and can retail for $150-250 depending on how you position it. The protocol includes scalp analysis and consultation, deep cleanse with the 9.1 Shampoo, scalp massage with targeted pressure points, conditioning treatment, and leave-in protection. We provide full training for spa teams - our founder Tina can do virtual education sessions, and we have comprehensive protocol guides.
Liz
The cruise environment is unique. We have guests from all over the world, different water conditions at each port, sun exposure on deck. How does your product address that?
Jade
That's actually our core positioning - environmental scalp health. The formula was specifically designed to address hard water damage, UV stress, and pollution. Your guests are exposed to different water qualities at every port, intense sun on deck, and air-conditioned environments that stress the scalp. Our products were built for exactly this use case.
Liz
What about retail? Can guests purchase products after their treatment?
Jade
Absolutely. That's where the model becomes really powerful. After a guest has an incredible scalp treatment, they want to continue at home. We have travel-friendly sizes - 100ml shampoo and conditioner at $27.50 retail, 50ml leave-in at $30 - perfect for onboard retail and guest purchases. Your spa becomes a retail profit center, not just a service center.
Liz
What are the margins looking like?
Jade
We typically work with professional partners at 50% off retail, so your cost would be around $27.50 for the full-size products retailing at $55. For backbar, the 1-liter is $75 cost, $150 retail. We're flexible on terms for the right hospitality partners.
[Transition to Close]
Liz
This is interesting. Our guests are exactly the demographic for premium scalp care - they're health-conscious, they have money to spend, and they're looking for unique experiences. A dedicated head spa treatment could differentiate us from other cruise lines.
Jade
Exactly. And the beauty of cruise is you have a captive audience for 7-14 days. They can experience the treatment, see results, and purchase products all in one voyage. It's the perfect environment to introduce something new.
Liz
What would a pilot look like?
Jade
I'd suggest starting with 2-3 ships. We'd provide full product kits for the spa teams, comprehensive training either virtual or in-person, treatment protocols, and marketing materials. We could do a 90-day pilot and measure: treatment bookings, retail sales, and guest feedback.
Liz
And the investment?
Jade
For a pilot, we could put together a starter kit at a reduced initial order - maybe $2,500-3,500 per ship including backbar and retail inventory. We'd provide training at no additional cost. The goal is proving the concept works for your guests.
Liz
Let me discuss with my team. Can you send me a formal proposal with product details, pricing, and training options?
Jade
Absolutely. I'll have that to you within 48 hours. Can I also send you samples so you can experience the product yourself? Once you feel the difference, I think you'll understand why our salons are so loyal.

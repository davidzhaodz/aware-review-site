The weather is shifting, and so is your scalp. Here's what's actually happening - and how to stay ahead of it.

Why Spring Catches Your Scalp Off Guard

Most people think spring is "easy" on the scalp. But the sudden humidity swing - going from dry indoor heat to warmer, moisture-heavy air - actually triggers a recalibration period where sebum production, scalp pH, and the microbiome all shift at once.

The result? A 2-4 week window where your scalp can feel oilier than normal, more reactive, or just "off" - even if your routine hasn't changed.

Seasonal Transition Signals

🌱

Oilier roots, drier ends

Your sebaceous glands are recalibrating to higher humidity. This usually self-corrects in 2-3 weeks if you don't over-cleanse.

💨

Extra shedding

Seasonal shedding is real. Hair follicles naturally cycle more in spring. It's usually temporary, not thinning.

☀️

Increased sensitivity or redness

UV exposure ramps up before you notice it. The scalp barrier, still recovering from winter dryness, is more vulnerable to sun and pollen.

The 3-Step Spring Reset

Simple adjustments to help your scalp transition smoothly.

1

Resist the urge to wash more

When oil production spikes, over-cleansing triggers a rebound cycle. Stick to your normal wash frequency and let your scalp recalibrate.

2

Focus on the scalp, not just the hair

Use fingertips (not nails) to gently massage cleanser into the scalp for 60 seconds. This loosens buildup and stimulates healthy circulation as follicles enter a new growth phase.

3

Lighten up your leave-in

If you've been using heavier products for winter dryness, now's the time to scale back. A lightweight leave-in that hydrates without weighing down is ideal for the humidity shift.

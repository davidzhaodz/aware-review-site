Approved Claims

What you can and cannot say when pitching to editors. All claims are tiered by evidence strength.

Tier 1

Clinical Data (Ingredient-Level Studies)

Backed by published clinical and laboratory data from AWARE's ingredient suppliers (Symrise, BASF). These are ingredient-level studies, not AWARE-specific product studies.

SymHair Force 1631 (Biofermented Microalgae)
  • 10.5% increase in anagen (growth-phase) hair
  • 82% improvement in breakage resistance
  • 59-63% decrease in apoptotic cells (ex vivo)
Source: Symrise, 30-volunteer clinical trial, 3 months
ResistHyal (Dual-Weight Hyaluronic Acid)
  • ~189x more hydration vs placebo (ex vivo)
  • +61% softness improvement after 28 days
  • +48% volume after 28 days
  • +46% shininess vs placebo
  • -65% reduction in brittle hair
  • -50% pollution particle adhesion
  • -15% straightening time
Source: BASF, multiple studies (71 volunteers + ex vivo)
How to frame: "AWARE's proprietary AWAREBIO Complex features biofermented microalgae clinically shown to support a 10.5% increase in growth-phase hair over three months." Never say "AWARE grows hair" or "AWARE is clinically proven."

Tier 4

Anecdotal / Testimonial

Customer and stylist-reported outcomes. Can be used as quotes or paraphrased, never as brand claims.

  • "Reduced hair fall within one month"
  • "Visible new growth within three months"
  • "Scalp felt calmer after first use"
  • Salon partners report clients asking for the product by name
Attribution required: "Customers report..." or "Salon partners have observed..." Never as a brand claim.
Off-Limits

Never Say These

Medical / Drug Claims
  • Never use: "cures," "treats," "heals," "prevents," "diagnoses"
  • Never use: "clinically proven to regrow hair"
  • Never use: "treats seborrheic dermatitis" or any named condition
  • Never use: "FDA approved" or imply FDA endorsement
Ingredient Errors
  • Do NOT list biotin as a current ingredient (roadmap only, not in current formula)
  • Do NOT list niacinamide as a current ingredient (roadmap only)
  • Never use "27% growth-phase increase" (corrected figure is 10.5%)
Distribution
  • Never suggest Amazon, Sephora, Ulta, Target, or mass retail availability
  • No urgency language ("limited time," "selling fast," "while supplies last")

The brand currently partners with 50+ salons, head spas, and clinics across Los Angeles, Chicago, and New York, with a 96% B2B repurchase rate and 0% product return rate.

Language Guide

Quick reference for how to frame claims in pitches and editor conversations.

Don't say

"AWARE Hair is clinically proven to improve scalp health"

Say instead

"AWARE Hair features clinically studied ingredients shown to support scalp health"

Don't say

"Our products stop hair loss"

Say instead

"Our proprietary complex includes biofermented microalgae associated with a 10.5% increase in growth-phase hair in clinical studies"

Don't say

"AWARE treats dandruff"

Say instead

"AWARE supports a healthier scalp environment through daily proactive care"

Don't say

"Contains biotin and niacinamide"

Say instead

"Formulated with biofermented Tahitian microalgae, dual-weight hyaluronic acid, and vegan keratin amino acids"

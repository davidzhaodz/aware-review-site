Who she is

Carrie is owner/CEO of TRIMM beauty + barber (legal: Norcal Salon Services, LLC), a professional-only beauty distributor, not a salon. She ran out of Central Point, Oregon for 17 years, closed that location in March 2026, and relocated the hub to Sacramento in February. She is actively rebuilding her brand partner roster and describes the move as "coming home." Single location, hands-on, relationship-driven voice on social.

Current brands: J Beverly Hills, ABBA, All Nutrient, Dikson, Pure Brazilian, Product Club, Tocco Magico, TRYBE. Color, chemical, and styling heavy. No scalp-care brand in the lineup.

Strategic read

The fit is real

  • Scalp care is a clean category gap, not a displacement fight
  • Her Sacramento model leans hard on in-store brand education events. AWARE's education-first approach maps well.
  • Timing is ideal. She is signing new partners for the relaunch right now.
  • Noah's intro carries weight, he is a respected distribution voice.

Candid take, don't skip

TRIMM is a small regional distributor still in transition. Even if this closes, the addressable reach is "licensed pros walking into one Sacramento storefront." Useful, not transformational. SSG or Sola-scale plays touch thousands of chairs, not dozens.

Treat this as a 15-minute discovery call to learn her distributor model and keep Noah's intro in good standing, not as a priority deal. Do not drop pricing, MOQs, or contract structure today.

Script

Opening · 30 seconds

"Hi Carrie, thanks so much for making time. Noah spoke highly of you and the Sacramento move. Before we dive in, I'd love to learn a bit about how TRIMM works on your side, and then I can share where AWARE is at. Does that work?"

Let her talk first. She's a relationship person, she'll appreciate it. Don't pitch yet.

If she asks "so tell me about AWARE" · 60-90 seconds

"AWARE is a professional-exclusive scalp care line. My co-founder Tina is a stylist and trichologist, she's been behind the chair for 40-plus years, and the whole line is built around what she kept seeing on scalps that standard haircare misses."

"We launched in August, salon-exclusive by design. No Amazon, no Sephora, no mass retail. Two core rituals, a proactive daily system and a scalp sanctuary treatment. We also do a sandalwood gua sha for scalp stimulation that salons are adding as a service."

"We're just starting distributor conversations now, and Noah thought you'd be someone worth learning from on how the distribution side actually works."

Do NOT lead with: reorder rate, revenue, salon count. Save for if she presses on traction. If she does ask: "We're growing quickly, mostly on the West Coast, and the repeat ordering from salons has been strong enough that we're starting to think about how to support them outside of California."

Pivot to discovery · then listen

"I'd love to understand TRIMM from your side. I've only seen what's public, so fill me in. What does the typical TRIMM customer look like? Independent salons, booth renters, barbershops?"

From here, ride her answers. Pick 2-3 follow-up questions from the list below depending on where the conversation goes.

Follow-up questions (pick 2-3, don't interrogate)

  1. "How did you land on Sacramento specifically for the relocation?"
  2. "How do you think about adding a new brand to the lineup? What gets you excited vs. what makes you pass?"
  3. "You've clearly built education into the model. What does a brand activation look like when it goes well?"
  4. "What's missing in your current roster that you wish you could offer?" (scalp may come up organically)
  5. "How do you split time between Sacramento customers and the Oregon pros you still support?"

Listening signals

Green · Lean in

  • Mentions scalp, sensitive skin, or wellness as a gap
  • Asks about margin structure or pro-only enforcement (she's serious)
  • Names specific salons or pros who'd be a good first fit
  • Brings up education events as a mutual interest

Amber · Slow down

  • Gets vague about how many pros she actually serves
  • Asks about bulk pricing without a plan
  • Wants marketing support or samples before any commitment signal
  • Compares AWARE to a brand she already dropped

Red · Walk away soft

  • Wants exclusive Sacramento rights on this first call
  • Pushes for big volume commitments upfront
  • Hints at mass retail or off-strategy channels
  • Asks to resell AWARE to consumers directly

Closing · last 2 minutes

If green

"This has been really helpful. Would it make sense to send over our wholesale one-pager and a small sample set so you can show Tina's story to a couple of your pros? Then we could reconnect in two weeks."

If uncertain

"I appreciate you walking me through the model. Let me sit with it and send you a short follow-up with a couple of next-step options, including doing nothing for now if the timing's not right."

If clear no

"Totally understand. Would you be open to me circling back in six months when we've added East Coast distribution? And thank you for Noah, I'll let him know the call was valuable."

Send / don't send

OK to send after

  • Wholesale one-pager (Bigelow line sheet format, not the luxury spa version)
  • Short brand intro email with Tina's story and the Four Pillars framework
  • Complimentary sample pair (Shampoo + Conditioner) for her team

Do not send

  • Specific pricing or MOQ numbers
  • Revenue or reorder-rate stats
  • Anything marked internal-only
  • Language implying TRIMM is "behind" or "missing" something
